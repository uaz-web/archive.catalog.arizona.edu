The Department of Near Eastern Studies provides undergraduate and graduate programs of study in the history, cultures, languages, and geography of the geopolitical region of the world currently referred to as the Middle East or the Near East in antiquity.

The department offers a major in Near Eastern studies for the Bachelor of Arts, Master of Arts, and Doctor of Philosophy degrees. For graduate admission and degree requirements, consult the Graduate Catalog.

The major for the Bachelor of Arts degree requires a minimum of 35 units including two years or the equivalent of Arabic, Persian, Hebrew, Akkadian or other appropriate language. All courses are chosen in consultation with and approved by a departmental advisor.

Students who intend to major in Near Eastern Studies should chose MATH 121 or MATH 117 (or equivalent) or a more advanced mathematics course to complete their mathematics requirement.
