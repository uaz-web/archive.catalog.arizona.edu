All courses below are approved to be taught in Summer 2003; however, some (or all) may not be offered.  The course numbers that are offered, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes. Classes with alternative delivery modes (Web based, cable TV, correspondence, etc) are noted in the Schedule at the section level.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).  For explanations of course elements see the Key to Course Descriptions.

SPAN 195A -- Topics in Spanish Culture, Literature, and Language  (1 unit)
Description:  This course will profile a variety of topics dealing with the study of the Spanish language and the culture and literature of the Spanish-speaking world. Intended for first-year students, the course will offer an introduction to the Spanish major and allow students to explore ways in which the study of Spanish can complement and enhance different career options. An assortment of reading selections and multimedia sources will be used; topics will cover select areas of linguistics, language and society, literature, history, current events, and the arts. Course will be conducted in English.  This is a First-Year Colloquium Course.
Usually offered:  Fall.

Description:  May be taken up to four times and will rotate between the following four topics.
