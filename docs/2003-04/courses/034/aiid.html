Fall 2003 Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Fall 2003; however, some (or all) may not be offered this term.  The course numbers that are offered this term link to the Schedule of Classes.  Classes with alternative delivery modes (Web based, cable TV, correspondence, etc) are noted in the Schedule at the section level.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).  For explanations of course elements see the Key to Course Descriptions.

Arizona International College: Interdisciplinary (AIID)  Department Info

AIID 101 -- On Becoming an Educated Person  (2 units)
Description:  Introduction to discourse on what it means to be college educated and the unique system by which AIC facilitates that discourse. Students will investigate not only the American education system but will also develop a more global approach to alternative systems and how a global ethos should be part of any educated person's thinking.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 102 -- Introduction to a Global Perspective  (3 units)
Description:  This course introduces students to a global perspective and to what it means to be a global thinker. It emphasizes three components of the perspective: historical origins, contemporary ramifications and manifestations, and the affective ways people communicate with one another. Involves media, guest speakers, and field trips.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Course includes 1 or more field trips.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 104 -- Statistical Techniques for Problems of the Modern World  (3 units)
Description:  The students will learn techniques of descriptive statistics and statistical inference including: organization and summarization of data; measures of central tendency and dispersion; probability; discrete and continuous random variables; point and interval estimation; hypothesis testing, regression, correlation, and chi-square.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  college algebra or equivalent. Consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 105 -- Human Consciousness and the Formation of World Views  (3 units)
Description:  This core course is an interdisciplinary exploration of the nature of consciousness, its historical, scientific and cultural meanings, and attendant world views. A comparative survey of early cosmology and mythology is followed by a review of modern epistemological shifts that have led to contemporary world views.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  or Concurrent registration, AIID 175A.
Typical structure:  1 hour discussion, 2 hours lecture.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 205 -- Global Issues in Ecology and Environment  (3 units)
Description:  Ecologists study interactions. We will learn about the theories, studies and research methods that have shaped our understanding of the natural world. We will study the effects of the planet's climate, geography and evolutionary history on the distributional patterns of life, interactions among life forms, and the structure /maintenance of biological ecosystems. We will also explore our own place within nature and the relevance of conservation ethics to the planet's future.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 206 -- Media, Arts and Society  (3 units)
Description:  The course will explore how popular and fine arts are affected by the mass media culture and act as agents of social identity and change in small- and large-scale societies. Concerns will be the study of how major forms of media evolved and the specific roles that fine and popular arts play in contemporary society.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 207 -- Global Political Economy  (3 units)
Description:  This course explores the interplay of national economies, political systems and development strategies at the global level. This course is part of the second-year core. Students and faculty will work together to investigate selected contemporary political/economic issues. Success in the course will rely heavily on students' critical, creative, systematic, and collaborative thinking and their use of communication, information, and technological skills.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  writing skills at sophomore level; consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 296A -- Projects Proseminar I: Connections  (2 units)
Description:  This seminar is an investigation that deepens students' knowledge of the topics addressed in the sophomore-level learning community and their powers of interdisciplinary thinking. It involves participation in 2nd year AIC learning community.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  participation in 1st-semester AIC learning community.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 296B -- Projects Proseminar: Connections II  (2 units)
Description:  "Connections" summarizes major points in the bodies of knowledge acquired while participating in the undergraduate core, illustrates the integration of the program, and provides opportunities for students to apply the knowledge and skills gained from the core experience. It involves participation in 2nd year AIC learning community.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  sophomore-level writing skills and basic technology skills. Participation in AIC 2nd-year learning community.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AIID 350 -- Conceptual Design  (3 units)
Description:  This course provides in-depth considerations of design as a philosophy of materials and information, structure, and form. History, case studies, and theories of design as evident within different fields, both locally and globally, will be explored, with special attention to how form and meaning develop from design processes.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  enrollment at AIC and 3rd-year standing, or consent of instructor.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 351 -- Technology and Information Culture  (3 units)
Description:  This course will examine the design, policy, and implementation strategies for information systems, particularly in the context of the development of digital communication technologies. There will be a focus on regional and global effects of information technologies on cultural, economic, social, and political issues.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AIID 352 -- Frontiers  (3 units)
Description:  Even before Star Trek we all knew that frontiers were arbitrary and unstable borders. This course examines the concept of frontiers from an interdisciplinary point of view. What are the frontiers of science today? What are those of art, literature, history? During the semester students question the process by which bodies of knowledge and cultural manifestations are legitimized. They will also examine the criteria that serve as the basis for including and excluding ideas, and define the intellectual and social stakes in making such determinations.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 353 -- Policy Analysis  (3 units)
Description:  This class considers the various tools for making policy. Using historical case studies as a backdrop, students will consider current issues in policy design, implementation, and evaluation protocols. The course will make use of tools from many disciplines to examine and evaluate the efficiency and effectiveness of policies.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AIID 396A -- Proseminar in Research Methods  (3 units)
Description:  Students will build on basic statistical and methodological skills learned in their required first year core courses. They will examine appropriate tools and methods necessary for approaching and solving social, economic, and environmental problems both locally and globally. This course will cover the various approaches used in at least two different academic disciplines.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  familiarity with basic statistical methods. Consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 396B -- Proseminar in Research Methods  (3 units)
Description:  Students will build on basic statistical and methodological skills learned in their required first year core courses. They will examine appropriate tools and methods necessary for approaching and solving social, economic, and environmental problems both locally and globally. This course will cover the various approaches used in at least two different academic disciplines.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.

Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AIID 434 -- Media Industries  (3 units)
Description:  Examination of a specific topic in media industries: ownership and concentration; media markets and industrial integration.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  M AR advanced standing.
Identical to:  M AR 434; M AR is home department.
Usually offered:  Fall.
