Summer 2004 Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Summer 2004; however, some (or all) may not be offered.  The course numbers that are offered, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes. Classes with alternative delivery modes (Web based, cable TV, correspondence, etc) are noted in the Schedule at the section level.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).  For explanations of course elements see the Key to Course Descriptions.

Arizona International College: Natural Science and Mathematics (AISM)  Department Info

AISM 120 -- College Algebra  (3 units)
Description:  This course for non-mathematics majors and minors covers the basic concepts of algebra, including linear equations and inequalities, relations and functions, quadratic equations, and systems of equations.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  two entrance units in algebra or an acceptable score on the math readiness test. Consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AISM 122 -- Biology of Organisms and the Environment  (4 units)
Description:  This course explores the functioning of plants and animals and how they adapt to and interact with their environment. Topics include physiology, genetics, development and reproduction, and ecology. The central organizing principle of the course is evolution as a way of understanding individual organisms and the diversity of life on earth. The course will include consideration of contemporary social issues such as health care and environmental ethics.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Course includes 1 or more field trips.
Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Spring.

Grading:  Alternative grades are awarded for this course: S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.

AISM 201 -- Statistical Techniques for Problems of the Modern World  (3 units)
Description:  This course covers the basic techniques of descriptive statistics and statistical inference, with applications related to the second-year learning community theme(s).
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  College Algebra skills.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AISM 220 -- Molecular Genetics in a Social Context: Darwin to Dolly  (3 units)
Description:  This course is intended to provide 200-level students with an integrated understanding of the genetics, biochemistry, molecular genetics and molecular biology that form the basis of the biological revolution caused by genetic engineering.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AISM 390A -- Special Topics: Tropical Biology  (3 units)
Description:  This course will introduce students to the biological, physical, chemical, and atmospheric conditions that have contributed to the incredible biodiversity of tropical wet and dry forests. Primary focus will be on the biology of biodiversity but considerataion will also be given to the socio-economic-political factors that impact healthy ecosystems. The course will be followed by an optional three-week summer field course in the coastal tropical rainforests of Corcovado National Park, Costa Rica.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  college-level intro to biology or ecology.
Typical structure:  2 hours lecture, 1 hour discussion.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AISM 390B -- Tropical Biology: Field Course  (3 units)
Description:  This is an upper-division science field course. The course will be held entirely on-site in Corcovado National Park, Costa Rica.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  physical ability to engage in extremely strenuous, unsupervised outdoor activity.
Usually offered:  Summer.

AISM 390C -- Natural History of the Sonoran Desert  (3 units)
Description:  A field and lecture course that will give the beginner and the seasoned field observer an introduction to the natural world of our region. Class experiences will include lectures, identification workshops, and field trips. Binoculars and participation in one or two 3-4 day field trips will be required.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  completion of AIC core or UA Gen Ed or consent of instructor.
Typical structure:  3 hours laboratory, 2 hours lecture.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.
