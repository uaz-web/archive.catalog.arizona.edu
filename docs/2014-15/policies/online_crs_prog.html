Academic Policies

Online Courses and Programs

Definitions

Fully Online Course:  All instruction takes place online with no required in-person activities with instructors or program staff.  Instruction may be asynchronous or it may be synchronous, which is often required for online meetings.  The course description will indicate whether synchronous activities or in-person proctored examinations are required.

Fully Online Program:  The instructional mode is completely online; however, program requirements may include in-person attendance at an orientation session and for proctored examinations, or independent off-line studies, a field trip, or an internship.  The independent studies, field trip, or internship will not require regular in-person contact with instructors or program staff.

Guiding Principles for Online Courses and Programs

  1. All online courses and programs reflect the same academic quality and standards as on-campus University courses and programs, regardless of the mode of instruction.
  2. University faculty members are responsible for online course and program content and quality.
  3. Admission and graduation standards for online programs are consistent with University policies and processes pertaining to on-campus courses and programs.
  4. Online degree and certificate programs bearing the same name as an on-campus degree or certificate program have the same content (with appropriate latitude for the distributed environment) and general requirements.  Students will achieve the same competencies and outcomes as those completing the same on-campus degree or certificate program.
  5. Official University transcripts do not differentiate courses or programs by mode of delivery.
