Arizona International College: Social Sciences (AISS)  Department Info

AISS 102 -- Economics of Sustainability  (3 units)
Description:  This course will introduce to students basic economic concepts, such as demand and supply, market structure, public goods, fiscal and monetary policies and growth, which can be used to study contemporary economic issues. Students will focus on the problem of sustainability and examine the interaction of economic policies, growth, sustainable resource use and the environment.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AISS 199 -- Independent Study  (1-3 units)
Description:  Qualified students working on an individual basis with professors who have agreed to supervise such work.
Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
May be repeated:  an unlimited number of times.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AISS 290A -- Special Topics: Theory and Practice in Non-profit Organizations  (3 units)
Description:  The objective of this course is to prepare students to intelligently engage in the ongoing debate about the role of non-profit organizations, as well as to prepare them for possible careers in the third sector. The course compares and contrasts the management, marketing and financial practices in non-profit organizations with for-profit organizations, and deals with the themes relevant to the history, political economy, organization and management of voluntary agencies.
Prerequisite(s):  Economics of Sustainability or Principles of Economics coursework.
Typical structure:  1 hour discussion, 2 hours lecture.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AISS 290C -- Special Topics in the Social Sciences: Nicaragua 2000  (3 units)
Description:  A combination of a 4-week study tour in Nicaragua (including a homestay and service experience), assigned readings, and an independent research project, geared to understanding Nicaragua's current situation as a Third World country coming to the international table of trade and democracy.
Prerequisite(s):  Spanish language ability equivalent to completion of at least one year of study.
Usually offered:  Fall.
