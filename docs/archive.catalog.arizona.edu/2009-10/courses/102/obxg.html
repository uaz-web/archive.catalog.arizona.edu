Summer 2010 Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Summer 2010; however, some (or all) may not be offered.  The course numbers that are offered, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes. Classes with alternative delivery modes (Web based, cable TV, correspondence, etc) are noted in the Schedule at the section level.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).  For explanations of course elements see the Key to Course Descriptions.

Obstetrics and Gynecology (OB G)  Department Info

OB G 581 -- Clinical Genetic Counseling  (2 units)
Description:  Principles of genetic counseling, general topics related to issues raised during genetic counseling (such as coping with chronic illnesses), and specific genetic counseling issues related to unique disorders encountered in the genetics clinic and other genetic counseling situations. Such disorders include prenatal, pediatric and adult genetic conditions.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  limited to students in the genetic counseling training program except by consent of instructor.
May be repeated:  for credit 6 times (maximum 7 enrollments).
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 585 -- Human Embryology and Teratology  (3 units)
Description:  A systems approach to embryonic and fetal development; principles of teratology with emphasis on specific teratogenic agents and counseling of exposed individuals.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  restricted to genetic counseling students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

OB G 594 -- Practicum  (1-3 units)
Description:  The practical application, on an individual basis, of previously studied theory and the collection of data for future theoretical interpretation.
Grading:  Alternative grades are awarded for this course: S P F.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring, Summer.

OB G 595A -- Introduction to Genetic Counseling  (1 unit)
Description:  Introduction to principles of genetic counseling including such issues as client autonomy, decision-making, risk perception, cultural sensitivity , and the techniques used to achieve these.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.
May be repeated:  for credit 1 time (maximum 2 enrollments).
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 596J -- Rounds  (1 unit)
Description:  The development and exchange of scholarly information, usually in a small group setting. The scope of work shall consist of research by course registrants, with the exchange of the results of such research through discussion, reports, and/or papers.
Grading:  Regular or alternative grades can be awarded for this course: A B C D E or S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  limited to students in the genetic counseling training program except by consent of instructor.
May be repeated:  for a total of 6 units of credit.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 813C -- Obstetrics & Gynecology Clerkship  (5-10 units)
Description:  In both inpatient and outpatient clinical settings, this course is an introductory experience in the provision of comprehensive medical care and counseling services to elderly, adult and adolescent female patients. The obstetrical conditions and gynecological problems commonly encountered by the physician provide the primary focus for this clerkship experience, but students may also be exposed to patients with serious or less common conditions. The basis for the clerkship is to introduce the clinical information thought to be fundamental in the education of all physicians. The clerkship is a required course for all medical students.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Successful completion of 1st & 2nd year’s medical school.
May be repeated:  for a total of 10 units of credit.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816A -- Extended Clinical Experience in Obstetrics  (1-4 units)
Description:  This elective will expand the clinical obstetric experience received by students during their MSIII OB/GYN clerkship. Students will participate in the antenatal assessment, intrapartum care, and delivery of obstetric patients at University Medical Center. Students will be given responsibilities above and beyond those of the clerkship, reflecting progression of knowledge and skills. Student experience during this elective may be tailored to reflect the specific interests of the student.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  OB G813C. Third-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816B -- Introduction to Perinatology  (1-4 units)
Description:  The student will participate actively in the care of women with “high-risk” pregnancies. This will include pregnancies complicated by maternal conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, thrombophilias, congenital heart disease, and advanced maternal age. Fetal complications such as structural anomalies, growth disturbances, or abnormal antenatal testing will also be included The student will develop an understanding of the roles of various methods of fetal evaluation (sonography, antenatal screening, amniocentesis), genetic counseling, and specialized medical care in caring for these women and their babies.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  OB G 813C. 3rd-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816C -- Women’s Geriatric Health and Pelvic Floor Medicine  (1-4 units)
Description:  This course will provide the student with an understanding of the health issues faced by women as they age. Issues such as pelvic relaxation, menopause, and incontinence will be addressed. The student will have the opportunity to interact with faculty and patients in both outpatient (medical management, pre-operative evaluation) and inpatient (surgical, peri-operative) settings.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  OB G 813C. Third-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816D -- Introduction to Gynecologic Oncology  (1-4 units)
Description:  This course will provide the student with an enhanced understanding of the evaluation and care of patients with gynecological cancers. The student will be an active member of the gynecological oncology patient care team. Learning will occur in both inpatient (surgical) and outpatient (medical and peri-operative) settings.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  OB G 813C. 3rd-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816E -- Family Planning/Reproductive Choices  (1-4 units)
Description:  This elective will allow students to get a more in-depth exposure to all aspects of family planning. Students will gain skills in counseling women in the use of birth control and various birth control methods, sexual health, and STD prevention, and options counseling. If the student chooses, (s)he will learn about the risks, benefits and alternatives of pregnancy termination, both medical and surgical. Students will get hands-on experience with pelvic exams and both transvaginal and transabdominal ultrasound. They will also become familiar with placement of IUDs and Implantation.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Third-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816F -- Introduction: Ultrasound in Obstetrics & Gynecology  (1-4 units)
Description:  The student will become familiar with the use of ultrasound (both transabdominal and transvaginal) in the evaluation of pelvic anatomy, pathology and pregnancy. He/she will gain an appreciation of the role of ultrasound in prenatal diagnosis and subsequent genetic counseling. The student will additionally observe special procedures such as sonohysterography, fetal echocardiography and ultrasound-guided amniocentesis.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Third year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816G -- Introduction to Genetic Counseling in Obstetrics & Gynecology  (1-4 units)
Description:  This course will provide the student with an understanding of the role of genetic counseling on obstetrics and gynecology. Issues such as preconceptional health, patient education/support, and various screening and/or diagnostic studies in pregnancy will be addressed. The student will also gain and understanding of the role of genetic counselors as a community resource for health care provider education and support.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  3rd-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall.

OB G 816H -- International Women’s Health  (1-4 units)
Description:  The student will partner with a faculty member to provide health care for women in an international setting. By direct participation, the student will gain an appreciation for the complexity of factors influencing women’s health internationally. Destination and clinical activities will vary with each health care project.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Third-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school. This elective requires identification of mentoring faculty member prior to enrollment.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816I -- Experience Structural Determinants of Women’s Health  (1-4 units)
Description:  This is an elective in which students “shadow” patients from underserved and minority communities. Students will also participate in a series of seminars addressing issues of medical sociology and the role of the community as a partner in health care. The learning objectives include: a) Identifying the political, environmental and cultural factors impacting patient health, policy, health systems and practice as they relate to women’s health issues. b) Identifying the social, environmental and economic barriers and social factors which facilitate and those which inhibit good patient outcomes. c) Developing an understanding of the causes and impact of health disparities in women’s health.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Third-year medical students. Successful completion of 1st & 2nd-year medical school.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

OB G 816L -- Introduction To Obstetrical Ultrasound  (1-10 units)
Description:  The purpose of this elective is to introduce the student to the fundamentals of obstetrical ultrasonography and is an ambulatory experience. This is primarily an observational experience. It is suitable for an individual who might have an interest in obstetrics and gynecology as a career or radiology as a career. While there is minimal hands on experience there is an opportunity to review and discuss findings and participate in didactic sessions and read the details of interesting ultrasonographic findings.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  3rd-year medical students with successful completion of 1st & 2nd-years of medical school.
Usually offered:  Spring.

OB G 816M -- Introduction To Reproductive Endocrinology  (1-10 units)
Description:  The purpose of this rotation which is predominantly an in hospital experience will be to discuss and observe complex surgical procedures related to female pelvic malignancy. There will be significant teaching and patient discussions which the medical student can participate in. This rotation is established to attract those individuals who feel that they might be interested in a career in obstetrics and gynecology or gynecologic oncology or those surgical specialties related to female pelvic surgery.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  3rd-year medical students with successful completion of 1st & 2nd-Years of medical school.
Usually offered:  Spring.

OB G 816N -- Introduction To Obstetrical Anesthesia  (1-10 units)
Description:  This is an elective which will be primarily observational to begin to learn the principles of anesthesia as they relate to obstetrical patients. This is an in hospital experience that is suitable for those who might be interested in a career in obstetrics and gynecology or anesthesia as a career. The student will be introduced to the principles of anesthesia and how those are integrated into the management of not only normal obstetric patients but also those individuals who are high of acuity with multiple obstetrical and/or medical complications.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  year medical students with successful completion of 1st & 2nd years of medical school.
Usually offered:  Spring.

OB G 891G -- Learning Through Pregnancy  (1-16 units)
Description:  This elective is intended to provide medical students with the opportunity to experience longitudinal care by following a woman throughout her pregnancy and neonatal period. The goal is for students to experience continuity of care with an emphasis on women’s health. The students will meet this goal by becoming familiar with the prenatal exam (the questions that are asked during different stages of the pregnancy and the concerns/risks that exist during various trimesters), becoming knowledgeable about normal prenatal care and the process of labor and delivery, and ultimately, learning about pregnancy from the prenatal patients themselves.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  open to 1st and 2nd-year Medical students only.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring, Summer.
