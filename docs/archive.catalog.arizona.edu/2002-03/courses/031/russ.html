RUSS 414B -- Russian Through Films  (1-3 units)
Temporary course:  offered during Spring 2003 only.
Description:  This is a Russian language course on Russian film open to and intended for upper-division Russian students. One of the mantras of artistic policy in the former Soviet Union was a quote, attributed to its first leader Lenin, that cinema is "the most important art of all." Our course on Russian Film will focus on modern and recent films from Russia and the former Soviet Union. We begin with a historical overview of the early Soviet cinema, and quickly proceed through representative films to the movies of the last decade. We will focus on the content, stories and messages of these films, but consider their style and technique as well.
Prerequisite(s):  Upper division level Russian.
May be repeated:  for credit 2 times (maximum 3 enrollments).
