The School of Journalism is in the College of Social and Behavioral Sciences.

The school's program is designed to balance a student's development in the theory and practice of journalism with an even stronger emphasis on the humanities, arts and sciences.  The school offers instruction in the reporting, writing and editing skills necessary for a journalism career along with opportunities for internships for professional development.  The school offers programs combining the major in journalism with a second major in Middle Eastern studies, Latin American studies or other programs with a global emphasis. A minor is required.

The School of Journalism is accredited by the Accrediting Council on Education for Journalism and Mass Communications.
