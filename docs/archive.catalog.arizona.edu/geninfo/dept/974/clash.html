CLASSICS

(CLAS/GRK /LAT )

The cultural environment of Greece and Rome is the subject matter taught in the classics department. Courses are given in the language, literature (in the original and in translation), art and archaeology and in the development and heritage of these civilizations.

The department offers a degree of Bachelor of Arts with majors in Greek, Latin, and classics and a degree of Master of Arts with a major in classics with concentrations in ancient Greek, Latin, or classical archaeology. The B.A. with a major in Greek, Latin or classics assumes general knowledge of mathematics; students must complete MATH 122. Programs leading to a Bachelor of Arts in Education and a Master of Education with a teaching major in Latin are also available. In addition, a number of the department's courses may be used toward a supporting minor in other graduate programs.
