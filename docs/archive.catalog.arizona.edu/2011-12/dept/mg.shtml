The College:

The college offers professional education in business administration. Its purpose is to prepare students for professional positions in the public and private sectors. The faculty are both scholars and practicing professionals representing industry, government, administration, health care, education, and transportation. The college is a member of the American Assembly of Collegiate Schools of Business, and its undergraduate and graduate curricula in business are accredited by the assembly.

Professional Admission (formerly Advanced Standing):

Initial admission to the Eller College does not guarantee entry to the professional program. The professional admission policy (below) restricts all enrollments in upper-division (300- and 400-level) courses taught by the departments in the College.

In order to be admitted into the professional program, Eller College students must:

  1. have credit for a minimum of 54 units, including all stipulated lower-division requirements;
  2. have a grade-point average of not less than a 2.75 in order to make application;
  3. have an approved application on file with the Eller College Undergraduate Programs Office under the professional admission policy; apply by the stated deadline date and comply with application procedures;
  4. complete an admissions interview and complete a skills assessment;
  5. attend the professional admission orientation.

Transfer Course Work:

All transfer course work applicable to the B.A. or B.S.B.A. degree requirements taken while enrolled in other colleges or at other universities is subject to acceptance by the College for degree certification purposes.

For more information contact the college office listed above.
