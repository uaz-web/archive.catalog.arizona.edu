The Critical Languages Program is in the School of International Languages, Literatures, and Cultures within the College of Humanities.

The program provides less commonly taught languages (LCTLs) which are not available in traditional departments or formats at the University of Arizona. Criteria for the introduction of new languages are (1) student, university or community need, (2) availability of native language tutors, and (3) proper audiolingual instructional materials. Sections vary in size from four to seven students.

Languages recently offered are Cantonese, Chechen, Hindi, Irish-Gaelic, Kazakh, Korean, Kurdish, Norwegian, Polish, Scots-Gaelic, Swahili, Swedish, Tagalog, Thai, Ukrainian and Vietnamese. Additional languages (African, North and South American, Asian, European) will be offered in response to student, University or community need.

For more information contact the program office listed above.
