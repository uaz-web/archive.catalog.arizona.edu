The Department of Neuroscience is in the School of Mind, Brain and Behavior in the College of Science.

The Department provides instruction on the development, organization, evolution, and function of nervous systems. Students engage in research projects related to the disciplines of cellular, developmental, molecular and systems neurobiology, neuroethology, and neurogenetics, targeting contemporary issues such as intra- and intercellular signaling, synaptic mechanisms, development of the central nervous system, information processing in sensory systems, insects as vectors of disease, mental retardation, evolution of neural systems, and biorobotics.
