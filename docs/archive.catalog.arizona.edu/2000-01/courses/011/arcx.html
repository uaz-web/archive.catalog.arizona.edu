Spring 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

NEW Feature:  Only courses offered in Spring 2001 link to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Spring 2001; however, some may not be offered this term.  Only courses offered this term are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

School of Architecture (ARC )  Dept Info - College Info

ARC 100 -- Testing Testing (3) I (Identical with RCSC 100, which is home).

ARC 101 -- Foundation Studio I (4) I Visual, haptic and cognitive representation by means of freehand drawing and material manipulation. 2R, 4S.

ARC 102 -- Foundation Studio II (4) II Visual, haptic and cognitive representation by means of technical drawing, descriptive geometry and material manipulation. 2R, 4S. P, ARC 101.

ARC 104 -- Sonora (3) II Multi-disciplinary intro to the Sonoran Region of Southern Arizona and Northwest Mexico. Through a series of presentations by scholars, scientists, poets, popular writers, architects and passionate observers, the course engages students with meaningful dialogue concerning one's position/obligation to a specific place. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only.

ARC 195A -- Freshman Colloquium (1) I II Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 201 -- Design Studio I: Composition (6) I Making of space by elements in motion and experiments probing the nature of materials. Introduction of spatial elements (line, plane, volume) and tectonic elements (material, arrangement, light). Fees. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 202 -- Design Studio II: Performance (6) I This course explores relationship of human experience and spatial and formal characteristics of architecture. The studio emphasizes development of design processes including value and goal identification, design concept formation, modeling techniques, and evaluation procedures. Fees. Field Trips. P, ARC 201.

ARC 221 -- Building Technology I (3) I Introduction to the basic principles in structure, materials and methods of construction and environmental control systems. 3R, 2S. P, ARC 102 or consent of instructor. Open to majors only.

ARC 222 -- Building Technology II (3) II Three-module course on the topics: statics and material principles, introduction to wood systems, elements of environmental control systems and human perception. 3R, 2S. P, ARC 221. Open to majors only.

ARC 227 -- Architectural Programming (2) II Programming and design methodologies including problem seeking, goal identification, code search, observation, questioning, relationship diagrams, brainstorming, space allocation, and simulation as techniques for processing information for building design. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 231 -- History I: World Architecture: Ancient Through Medieval (3) I Considers the creation, use and interpretation of ancient and medieval architecture from a variety of perspectives: environmental, functional, material, structural, formal, socio-political, and cultural.

ARC 232 -- History II: World Architecture, Renaissance Through Modern (3) II Considers the creation, use and interpretation of architecture from the fifteenth century through the twentieth from a variety of perspectives: environmental, functional, material, structural, formal, socio-political, and cultural. P, open to majors only.

ARC 241 -- Design Communication I (3) I Course emphasis is on the development of basic communication techniques for the study and presentation of architectural concepts. 2R, 4S. P, ARC 101, ARC 102; CR, ARC 201. Admission to professional phase.

ARC 294 -- Practicum (1-4) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 299 -- Independent Study (1-4) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 299H -- Honors Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 301 -- Design Studio III: Land Ethics (6) I Explores relationship of architecture to site and context. Explores design in relation to issues like topography, climate, vegetation, wildlife, hydrology, historical context, and regional materials. Design sites may include both urban and rural sites. Fees. P, ARC 202.

ARC 302 -- Design Studio IV: Tectonics (6) II Design of buildings with emphasis on tectonic assembly and spatial integration. Fees.

ARC 318 -- Elements of Structural Systems (3) I Force systems in equilibrium, introductory mechanics of materials, response of structural elements to stresses, principles of structural design including general characteristics of structural hierarchies. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 321 -- Building Technology III (3) I Three-module course exploring technological issues relating to small and intermediate scale buildings; including environmental issues of climate/micro-climate and daylighting. 3R, 2S. P, ARC 222.

ARC 322 -- Building Technology IV (3) II Integration of building systems. 3R, 2S. P, ARC 321.

ARC 326 -- Site Analysis and Planning (2) I Introduction to the study of the context in which architecture is developed. Course will introduce students to the second of the pre-design studies essential to the understanding of architecture. P, ARC 202, ARC 227; CR, ARC 301.

ARC 328 -- Wood and Steel Structural Systems (3) II Analysis and design of structural components and systems constructed of wood and steel including joists, beams, and columns. Analysis and design of members under single and combined loads. Examination of the behavior of individual elements and the total system. P, ARC 318.

ARC 332 -- History III: World Architecture, Modern and Contemporary (3) II A study of modern architecture through a critical examination of particular works, in an attempt to locate the duration and effect of the era on the discipline of architecture. P, ARC 231; CR, ARC 232. Open to majors only.

ARC 335 -- Construction Systems (3) II Analysis of contemporary systems of building construction with emphasis on assembly and integration of components, construction procedures and sequences, understanding how buildings go together, introduction to codes and regulations. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 336 -- Environmental Control Systems (3) I Analysis of contemporary systems of environmental control, i.e. heating, ventilation, air conditioning, lighting, power distribution, plumbing and hygiene. Emphasizes integration of systems into buildings and understanding impact of systems on architectural design and each other. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 341 -- Design Communication II (3) I Course emphasis is on the further development of communication techniques for the study and presentation of architectural ideas. 4R, 2S. P, ARC 241; CR, ARC 301.

ARC 344 -- The Art and Architecture of the Islamic World (3) I II (Identical with NES 344, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Arts.

ARC 394 -- Practicum (3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 399 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 399H -- Honors Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] II Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 401 -- Design Studio V: Technology (6) I Content and theme will be design and programming of projects which grow and change with systematic clarity. Emphasis on future oriented problem identification, efficient utilization of resources, the appropriate interface with contextual activities and building systems. Fees. P, ARC 302. May be convened with ARC 501.

ARC 402 -- Design Studio VI: Urban Form (6) II Emphasis on urban context of architectural design with issues such as housing, community design, urban design, and urban infrastructure elements (transportation, recreation, education and utilities). Will study city as culture: the city as an environment for well-being. Fees. P, ARC 401. May be convened with ARC 502.

ARC 418 -- Concrete and Masonry Structural Systems (3) I Analysis/design of structural components and systems constructed of concrete and masonry including slabs, joists, beams, columns, retaining walls, and foundations. Analysis/design of members under single and combined loads using working stress and ultimate strength procedures. P, admission to professional phase.

ARC 421 -- Building Technology V (3) I Technological issues related to large scale, complex buildings, building code, life safety, design of passive and active environmental control systems. 3R, 2S. P, ARC 322.

ARC 422 -- Building Technology VI (3) II Simultaneous thinking about site when building in urban context, one and two-directional structural systems, alternative renewable energy and environmental indoor and outdoor climate modification systems. 3R, 2S.

ARC 428 -- Advanced Structural Systems (3) II Analysis and design of long span elements and structures subject to lateral loads. Evaluation of alternative systems. Methods of analysis for various systems from single story to high rise. Response of systems to wind and seismic loads. P, ARC 418.

ARC 432 -- History IV: Urban Form (2) I A lecture survey dealing with the origins and evolution of communal ordering systems. A variety of critical attitudes are developed with which current and historic settlement patterns are visually compared to discover spatial attributes as a dimension of human experience. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; ARC 302, grade of "Pass" for UDWPE or completion of ENGL 397A. (Identical with PLN 432). May be convened with ARC 532.

ARC 441 -- Construction Documents (3) I The study of the concepts, vocabulary, intent, and skills necessary to understand construction documents. The focus is on the analysis and creation of a set of working drawings that will illustrate the student's mastery of the material. P, ARC 302. May be convened with ARC 541.

ARC 451 -- Design Studio VII: Research Electives (6) I Studio work may include: desert architecture, commercial design, historic preservation, computer aided design, competitions, design/build, entrepreneurial design, architectural programming and evaluation, interdisciplinary studio architecture, planning and/or landscape architecture. All topics may not be offered each year. Fees. May be convened with ARC 551.

ARC 452 -- Design Studio VIII: Senior Project (6) II Studio-based project demonstrating a synthesis of knowledge or development of theoretical concepts, process and methodology, and definitive resolution of design issues. Fees. P, ARC 451.

ARC 452H -- Honors Design Studio VIII: Senior Project (6) Studio-based project demonstrating a synthesis of knowledge or development of theoretical concepts, process and methodology, and definitive resolution of design issues. P, ARC 451; admission to Honors Program.

ARC 459 -- Ethics and Practice (2) I Standards and values of architectural services and professional project and practice management. P, ARC 402. May be convened with ARC 559.

ARC 461A -- Solar Utilization in the Built Environment (3) I Survey of solar energy utilization principles, methods and case studies. May be convened with ARC 561A.

ARC 461B -- Lightweight Construction Techniques (3) II Survey of lightweight construction techniques, including pneumatics, tensile membranes, three-dimensional cable nets, grid shells and flexure stiff plates. May be convened with ARC 561B.

ARC 461C -- Tectonic Studies (3) I Objective is to gain understanding of relationship between architecture and technology, the interconnection of design, structure and materials. Discussion readings and discussions will focus on contemporary solutions to the perennial issues of craft, expression and honesty. 2D, 1R. P, ARC 322 or consent of instructor. May be convened with ARC 561C.

ARC 461D -- Computer Energy Analysis (3) I A comprehensive course that teaches students energy conservation and passive solar architecture and up-to-date computer energy simulation techniques. The course promotes students learning through field investigation of existing buildings and/or new design projects. P, ARC 302. May be convened with ARC 561D.

ARC 461E -- Advanced Computer Energy Analysis (3) [Rpt./ 1] II Lectures on advanced passive solar soft technology features fine tune passive architectural designs. Explanation of thermal performance/alternative applications on a basecase design. Thermal efficiency/energy cooling/heating loads assessed through computer energy simulation. P, ARC 461D. May be convened with ARC 561E.

ARC 461G -- Environmental Efficiency in High-Rise Buildings (3) I Explores possibilities, challenges and potentials of low-tech strategies and techniques, like natural ventilation and thermal storage in high-tech applications, focusing on building type of high-rise office tower and possibilities for future high-rise residential buildings. 2R, 2S. P, ARC 402. May be convened with ARC 561G.

ARC 471A -- Architecture and Planning in Mexico (3) I Study of architectural development in Mexico during the prehispanic, Spanish colonial and contemporary periods, with emphasis on design ideas from each period. (Identical with LA S 471A). May be convened with ARC 571A.

ARC 471B -- Space: A Social Cultural View (3) I Explores theoretical distinctions between processes of social and symbolic space, i.e. sacred ritual, rhetorical territoriality and local ritual. Extrinsic, expressive forms in architecture and landscape serve social ends and are distinguished from more intrinsic aesthetics. P, consent of department. May be convened with ARC 571B.

ARC 471C -- Critical Regionalism (2-4) II Seminar explores variety of interpretations of critical regionalist theory as applied to built environment. Selected writings read and discussed. Students construct own interpretations of general theory and focus on specific region to develop research paper. P, open to majors only. May be convened with ARC 571C.

ARC 471D -- Evaluating Environmental Experience (3) II This course concerns the significance of physical settings to immediate human experience. Explores the importance of form to experiences of wayfinding, visual and non-visual aesthetics, task performance, territoriality, and cultural expression. May be convened with ARC 571D.

ARC 471E -- American Architecture (3) II Analysis of American buildings, works of urban and regional infrastructure and works of art responding to clearly defined cultural and environmental conditions. P, consent of instructor. Open to non-majors. May be convened with ARC 571E.

ARC 471F -- Introduction to Conservation of Cultural Resources (3) I An overview of the Historic Preservation movement in America, including discussion of concepts, rationale for and methods of resource utilization, implementation of plans, legislation, etc. May be convened with ARC 571F.

ARC 471I -- Urban Space: History, Theory, Design (3) II Investigates a number of cities as historical case studies of issues informing design of urban public space: social construction of space, ethical positions on accommodation of individual and community in the city, role of memory and symbolism in creating sense of place, etc. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only. P, ARC 231, ARC 232.

ARC 474 -- Field Methods in Environmental Psychology (3) II P, PSYC 290A or PSYC 290B. (Identical with PSYC 474, which is home). May be convened with ARC 574.

ARC 481A -- Advanced Design Communication Applications (3) I II Two separate modules combine graphic communication techniques taught in earlier courses. Emphasizes use of freehand perspective, orthographic drafting, computer modeling and physical models, then uses computer as a tool to link different outputs into a seamless presentation. P, ARC 402. May be convened with ARC 581A.

ARC 481B -- Advanced Design Communication (3) I Course consists of two separate modules dealing with study of advanced communication theories and their application to architectural communication. Current theories and new technologies including computer graphics, video and interactive multi-media techniques will be discussed. P, ARC 402. May be convened with ARC 581B.

ARC 481C -- Communicating Design Data (3) I Concepts, vocabulary and skills to understand graphic communications in architecture, landscape architecture, and planning. Focus on analysis of information and creation of visual models illustrating mastery of existing material and researching new, emerging forms of graphic communication. P, ARC 341 or consent of instructor. May be convened with ARC 581C.

ARC 481D -- Architectural Photography (3) II Emphasis on the "daily use" of 35mm equipment and color slide films for self expression, documentation (exteriors/interiors), copywork, scale models and simulation. Introductory hands-on exploration of large format photography with Polaroid film. May be convened with ARC 581D.

ARC 481E -- Architecture in the Mediterranean (3) S Develop awareness of architecture, places and spaces in new cultures. Experience uses of space, materials, structural techniques and cultural expression. Highest priority placed on recording new and different experiences emphasizing creativeness, character and expression. May be convened with ARC 581E.

ARC 481G -- Computer Presentations in Multi-Media (3) II For students from visual arts with beginning knowledge of communicating with electronic media. Explore theory, principles, and strategies of designing with virtual images, animation, and sound. Focus on building interactive presentation using network technology/digital storage media. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only. May be convened with ARC 581G.

ARC 496D -- Mediterranean Cities in the 15th-16th c.: Cairo, Istanbul, Florence & Venice (3) II (Identical with NES 496D, which is home). May be convened with ARC 596D.

ARC 496T -- Technology Seminar (2) II Directed study focused on the technological development of the capstone project. May be convened with ARC 596T.

ARC 497A -- Research Methods (3) I II An interdisciplinary survey of research methods applicable to research in Architecture, Planning, and Landscape Architecture. P, 5th-year or graduate standing. May be convened with ARC 597A.

ARC 497B -- Special Projects in Architecture (1-3) [Rpt./ 6 units] Contact department for a description of this course. P, consent of department. May be convened with ARC 597B.

ARC 497C -- Light Fixture Design (3) II Course addresses logical principles of light fixture design related to conventional industrial design practices including geometry, material assembly and performance. Introduces lighting concepts related to environmental design including task, direct and indirect lighting. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only.

ARC 497D -- Design Development for Architectural Interiors (3) II This course will focus on the concept of designing public space environments that are compatible with the architecture envelope, context and structure of the building as well as accommodating human needs. Field Trips. P, ARC 301 or consent of instructor. May be convened with ARC 597D.

ARC 497E -- Furniture Design and Construction (3) I Introduction to designing, constructing, detailing and finishing furniture. Focus on safety, design theory, craftsmanship, functional requirements, material, and joinery. Use of materials and structure that meet programming/concept criteria for designing/constructing furniture. Fees. Field Trips.

ARC 497F -- Construction Laboratory (3) I A laboratory course focusing on materials, craftsmanship, tools, safety, tectonic theories, programmatic and functional requirements of designing, detailing and constructing full scale building components. P, at least 4th-year standing in professional program suggested. May be convened with ARC 597F.

ARC 497I -- Interdisciplinary Studio for Community Design (3-6) I Contact department for a description of this course. Field Trips. P, open to non-majors only. (Identical with PLN 497I). May be convened with ARC 597I.

ARC 499H -- Honors Independent Study (1-4) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

ARC 501 -- Design Studio V: Technology (6) I For a description of course topics see ARC 401. Graduate-level requirements include preparation of an extensive typological study and an extensive presentation which includes structural and bioclimatic response data. Fees. May be convened with ARC 401.

ARC 502 -- Design Studio VI: Urban Form (6) II For a description of course topics see ARC 402. Graduate-level requirements include greater responsibility in preparation of reading discussion material and demonstrating higher level of knowledge and production in studio work. Fees. May be convened with ARC 402.

ARC 532 -- History IV: Urban Form (2) I For a description of course topics see ARC 432. Graduate-level requirements include a more in-depth research background that could result in an extended final paper. (Identical with PLN 532). May be convened with ARC 432.

ARC 541 -- Construction Documents (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 441. Graduate-level requirements include presenting a construction document project in class or a paper describing CD techniques and procedures. May be convened with ARC 441.

ARC 551 -- Design Studio VII: Research Electives (6) I For a description of course topics see ARC 451. Graduate-level requirements include additional project development focusing on a particular aspect of the topic under study. Fees. May be convened with ARC 451.

ARC 559 -- Ethics and Practice (2) I For a description of course topics see ARC 459. Graduate-level requirements include a written 5 to 10 page research paper on an ethics and practice area as agreed to by the instructor. This will constitute 15% of the graduate student grade. May be convened with ARC 459.

ARC 560 -- Graduate Computer Modeling (3) I II Study and use of computing applied to the architecture graduate program including architectural graphics, desktop publishing, CAD, and computer presentations. Previous experience required with word processing, spreadsheets and the DOS and Macintosh operating systems. P, graduate admission.

ARC 561A -- Solar Utilization in the Built Environment (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 461A. Graduate-level requirements include additional graduate credit exercise. May be convened with ARC 461A.

ARC 561B -- Lightweight Construction Techniques (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 461B. Graduate-level requirements include an additional project demonstrating a comprehensive grasp of one lightweight construction technique. May be convened with ARC 461B.

ARC 561C -- Tectonic Studies (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 461C. Students registered for graduate credit are additionally expected to place the report topic within a historical context. 2D, 1R. May be convened with ARC 461C.

ARC 561D -- Computer Energy Analysis (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 461D. Graduate-level requirements include writing short essays on development exercises and presenting simple payback and lifecycle cost analysis on projects. May be convened with ARC 461D.

ARC 561E -- Advanced Computer Energy Analysis (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 461E. Graduate-level requirements include an additional research report on selected passive solar systems. May be convened with ARC 461E.

ARC 561G -- Environmental Efficiency in High-Rise Buildings (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 461G. Graduate-level requirements include grading on their research and presentation. This constitutes 10% of the quantitative grade. 2R, 2S. May be convened with ARC 461G.

ARC 571A -- Architecture and Planning in Mexico (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 471A. Graduate-level requirements include an additional research paper on a particular aspect of Mexican architecture. (Identical with LA S 571A). May be convened with ARC 471A.

ARC 571B -- Space: A Social Cultural View (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 471B. Graduate-level requirements include in-depth written discussions and in-depth paper or design projects applying the course content to some actual social setting. May be convened with ARC 471B.

ARC 571C -- Critical Regionalism (2-4) I II For a description of course topics see ARC 471C. Graduate-level requirements include developing in-depth research background that could reasonably result in a somewhat extended final paper. May be convened with ARC 471C.

ARC 571D -- Evaluating Environmental Experience (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 471D. Graduate-level requirements include critiques accounting for 10% each for the overall grade; greater weight is placed on prototype test which includes extensive, annotated bibliography for total of 50% of course grade. May be convened with ARC 471D.

ARC 571E -- American Architecture (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 471E. Graduate-level requirements include an additional research project that focuses on and develops one of the major topics of the course. May be convened with ARC 471E.

ARC 571F -- Introduction to Conservation of Cultural Resources (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 471F. Graduate-level requirements include an in-depth research paper focusing on a particular concept or methodology utilized in preservation practice. May be convened with ARC 471F.

ARC 574 -- Field Methods in Environmental Psychology (3) II (Identical with PSYC 574, which is home). May be convened with ARC 474.

ARC 581A -- Advanced Design Communication Applications (3) I II For a description of course topics see ARC 481A. Graduate-level requirements include a more sophisticated and professional product. May be convened with ARC 481A.

ARC 581B -- Advanced Design Communication (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 481B. Graduate-level requirements include one additional reading assignment, presentation of a short paper discussing the reading material and will be required to form a special team to prepare more extensive team project. May be convened with ARC 481B.

ARC 581C -- Communicating Design Data (3) I For a description of course topics see ARC 481C. Graduate-level requirements include preparing a manual intended as a teaching guide for undergraduates, further explaining techniques and procedures demonstrated in class. May be convened with ARC 481C.

ARC 581D -- Architectural Photography (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 481D. Graduate-level requirements include an additional assignment demonstrating ability to apply their knowledge of architectural photography to their academic work, prefaced by a written and oral proposal. May be convened with ARC 481D.

ARC 581E -- Architecture in the Mediterranean (3) S For a description of course topics see ARC 481E. Graduate-level requirements include a research paper. May be convened with ARC 481E.

ARC 581G -- Computer Presentations in Multi-Media (3) II For a description of course topics see ARC 481G. For students from visual arts with beginning knowledge of communicating with electronic media. Explore theory, principles, and strategies of designing with virtual images, animation, and sound. Focus on building interactive presentation using network technology/digital storage media. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only. May be convened with ARC 481G.
