The Program:

The Pharmacology and Toxicology GIDP is administered by the Graduate Interdisciplinary Programs Office.  The Department of Pharmacology is in the College of Medicine.  The Department of Pharmacology and Toxicology is in the College of Pharmacy.

The graduate program is interdisciplinary, with a faculty of 55, offering programs leading to the Master of Science and Doctor of Philosophy degrees in pharmacology and toxicology.  The Master of Science degree also offers a special concentration in Perfusion Science or Industrial Hygiene.  Faculty members have research interests in carcinogenesis/cancer chemotherapy, cardiovascular pharmacology, clinical pharmacology, endocrine pharmacology, environmental and biochemical toxicology, immunopharmacology, molecular/biochemical pharmacology, molecular toxicology, neuropharmacology, natural products chemistry, pharmacokinetics, and toxicology.

Effective Spring 2004, the Pharmacology and Toxicology GIDP splits and transfers to:  

The M.S. and Ph.D. with major in Pharmacology & Toxicology in the College of Pharmacy, Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences.
The M.S. and Ph.D. with major in Medical Pharmacology in the College of Medicine, Department of Pharmacology.  

For more information contact the program office listed above.
