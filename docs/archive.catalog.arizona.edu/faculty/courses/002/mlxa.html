Summer 2000 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses listed below are approved UA courses as of Summer 2000. Not all approved courses will be offered during this term. For course offerings/availability during a specific term, see the Schedule of Classes. Each course number below links to the Schedule of Classes.

Military Aerospace Studies (ML A)  Dept Info - College Info

ML A 100A First Year General Military Course, Air Force Today (2) I First Year GMC* Survey of the doctrine, mission, and organization of the U.S.A.F.; U.S. strategic offensive and defensive forces; U.S. general purpose and aerospace support forces. P, ML A 100A is not prerequisite to ML A 100B.

ML A 100B First Year General Military Course, Air Force Today (2) II First Year GMC* Survey of the doctrine, mission, and organization of the U.S.A.F.; U.S. strategic offensive and defensive forces; U.S. general purpose and aerospace support forces. P, ML A 100A is not prerequisite to ML A 100B.

ML A 110 Introductory Flight School (2) [Rpt./ 1] I II Introduction to the main fields of aircraft and airport operations. Topics included are basic aerodynamics, FAA regulations, radio procedures, human factors, cockpit management, meteorology, and interpretation of weather data and navigation.

ML A 200A General Military Course, History of Aviation (2) I Second Year GMC* Survey of the development of aviation from the advent of the air age to the present, with emphasis on military aviation and its relationship with political and economic aspects of historical world situations. P, ML A 200A is not prerequisite to ML A 200B.

ML A 200B General Military Course, History of Aviation (2) II Second Year GMC* Survey of the development of aviation from the advent of the air age to the present, with emphasis on military aviation and its relationship with political and economic aspects of historical world situations. P, ML A 200A is not prerequisite to ML A 200B.

ML A 250A Leadership Lessons from the Civil War (2) [Rpt./ 1] I II Series of general interest topics in leadership including leadership styles, relevance of historical lessons of leadership, and contemporary leadership challenges and approaches. Satisfies requirements of Arizona Blue Chip Program. Non-ROTC students are encouraged to enroll.

ML A 260A Transformational Leadership (2) [Rpt./ 1] I II Series of general interest topics in leadership including leadership styles, relevance of historical lessons of leadership, and contemporary leadership challenges and approaches. Satisfies requirements of Arizona Blue Chip Program. Non-ROTC students are encouraged to enroll.

ML A 300A Third Year Professional Officer Course, Leadership and Management (3) I Theory and application of leadership and management, with emphasis on human relations, motivation, communication for managers, organizational behavior and Total Quality Management (TQM) principles and processes. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 300B Third Year Professional Officer Course, Leadership and Management (3) II Theory and application of leadership and management, with emphasis on human relations, motivation, communication for managers, organizational behavior and Total Quality Management (TQM) principles and processes. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 400A Fourth Year Professional Officer Course, American Defense Policy (3) I Critical analysis of various aspects of the military in American society and its application and effects on the world political and economic environment. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 400B Fourth Year Professional Officer Course, American Defense Policy (3) II Critical analysis of various aspects of the military in American society and its application and effects on the world political and economic environment. P, consult department before enrolling.
