Baccalaureate Programs

The curriculum of the School of Information Science, Technology & Arts (SISTA) includes concepts and techniques that are essential in computational biology, computational linguistics, digital libraries, cognitive science and neuroscience, computer science and engineering, artificial intelligence and robotics, geospatial information management, social networks, and other disciplines. While students in both the B.S. and B.A. programs study a wide range of topics relating to the ways in which information is obtained, stored, organized, processed, and understood, they will differ in their focus:   

  • The Information Science and Technology B.S. Program is intended for students who expect to use their knowledge in a scientific job or postgraduate program, so the course work is more technical and has a solid mathematical and algorithmic basis. It requires advanced mathematics courses, more science courses, and more breadth in the SISTA disciplines.

  • The Information Science and Arts B.A. Program extends the idea of a liberal arts education. In the Information Age, a well-educated citizen must understand the interrelatedness of information science, technology and arts. This program requires less mathematics and fewer technical courses than the B.S.; however, it requires an area of emphasis—a minor.  Students are encouraged to select a minor in one of the fine arts, or in an area of the humanities or the social sciences.
