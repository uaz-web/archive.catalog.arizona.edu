Summer 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses offered in Summer 2001 are linked to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Summer 2001; however, some may not be offered this Summer.  The courses that ARE offered this Summer, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Retailing and Consumer Science (RCSC)  Dept Info - College Info

RCSC 114 -- Introduction to Retailing (3) I II Introduction to processes used for distributing consumer goods from manufacturers through wholesale and retail channels.

RCSC 115 -- Retail Design (3) Study of store planning, design, architecture, fixtures, merchandising, lighting, color, and materials. P, open only to majors or by consent of the department.

RCSC 193 -- Internship (1-12) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 199 -- Independent Study (1-3) Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 215 -- Retail Advertising and Promotion (3) II Activities used to influence sale of merchandise and services to promote trends and ideas; promotional plans including advertising, visual display, special events and publicity. P, or CR, RCSC 115.

RCSC 255 -- Visual Merchandising and Store Design (3) I S Study of the retail environment, the physical and psychological effects that initiate and motivate customer activity through visual merchandising and store design. P, RCSC 115 or ART 101.

RCSC 284 -- Product Development and Brand Strategies (3) I II Survey of soft and hard goods including product development and brand strategy. Topics include materials, manufacturing, design, specifications, performance, private branding strategies, brand management and marketing. P, 4 units of physical science.

RCSC 293 -- Internship (1-3) Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 294 -- Practicum (1-8) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 299 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 304 -- Merchandising Planning and Control (3) I Study of retail planning and control procedures with emphasis on retail mathematics and computer applications. P, MATH 121 or MATH 123: ACCT 200. Open to majors only.

RCSC 330 -- Food Retailing Principles (3) I Study of food retailing principles and promotional techniques unique to food retail establishments; inventory planning and control, buying, pricing, operation, human resources; current global issues affecting the food industry including production agriculture, manufacturing and wholesale distribution; strategic management and food consumption behavior. P, or CR, MKTG 361.

RCSC 340 -- Consumer Concepts and Theory (3) I Study of consumer behavior and the decision making processes involved when individuals or groups select, purchase, use or dispose of products and services to satisfy needs and desires; analysis of quantitative data using SPSS program. P, PSYC 230, PSYC 290 or SOC 274, SOC 275.

RCSC 350 -- Supply Chain Management (3) I A study of the supply side of retailing, focusing on purchasing, inventory management, physical distribution and the retail and supplier institutions in the retail channel. There is also focus on the selection, design, and management of effective channels. P, B AD 303, MKTG 361 or, CR, MKTG 361.

RCSC 376 -- Consumer Rights and Regulations (3) I Legal and ethical aspects of consumer and business rights and responsibilities in retailing and services. Roles of federal, state, local agencies and safeguards for consumer and business professionals are examined. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement.

RCSC 384 -- Leadership, Ethics, and Practice (3) I II Theories and applications of leadership and communication in the workplace. Examination of personal and professional qualities essential for successful internship and career planning. Roles, responsibilities and ethical standards for professionals will be examined. P, or CR, ENGL 307.

RCSC 393 -- Internship (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 394 -- Practicum (1-8) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 399 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

RCSC 400 -- Retail Strategy (3) Development, organization, implementation, and control of retail strategies in the context of the retail mix of product, price, promotion, and distribution. Case studies used to apply the concepts. P, ACCT 200, MKTG 361. (Identical with MKTG 400).

RCSC 411 -- Consumer Issues in Nutrition (3) S P, N SC 101 or N SC 301, ECON 201A or 201B. (Identical with N SC 411, which is home).

RCSC 424 -- Services Retailing (3) I An in-depth exploration of the marketing of profit-centered services to consumers, highlighting the distinct characteristics of services as compared to physical goods, and the special challenges posed by those characteristics. Strategies employed by service firms to effectively address these special conditions will be studied. P, MKTG 361. (Identical with MKTG 424). May be convened with RCSC 524.

RCSC 434 -- Non-store and Interactive Retailing (3) II Application of retail planning and control procedures with emphasis on development and evaluation of retail practices and strategies using the case method. P, MKTG 361. May be convened with RCSC 534.

RCSC 446 -- Global Retailing (3) II Analysis of international market environment and retailing structure, system, issues and trends in the global market, understanding global consumers and developing retail strategies. P, RCSC 114, MKTG 361; or CR, MKTG 361. May be convened with RCSC 546.

RCSC 451 -- Marketing Strategy (3) I Marketing experience that examines the marketing mix in the context of the multidimensional external environment relying substantially on the case study approach. The phases of analysis, planning, implementation, and control of the marketing function are covered. P, B AD 303; CR, B AD 303 or MKTG 361. (Identical with AREC 451).

RCSC 452 -- Business Policy (3) II Course integrates the functional areas of business and covers topics such as the strategic planning process, decision making under uncertainty, external environmental analysis, organizational analysis, competitive analysis, and social responsibility. P, open to majors only. (Identical with AREC 452).

RCSC 555 -- Visual Merchandising and Display (3) I S All aspects of displaying merchandise, including window display, interior display, color and lighting techniques, line and composition, three-dimensional presentation, fixtures and systems, planning and layout. P, RCSC 115 or ART 101.

RCSC 606 -- Theoretical Perspectives of Global Consumption and Retailing (3) I Advanced study of theory and research on global consumption, emphasizing cross-cultural consumer decision-making processes, and their relationship to retailers' strategy development in the international environment. P, RCSC 446 or equivalent; MKTG 361 or B AD 303.

RCSC 607 -- Topics in Retailing and Consumer Sciences (3) [Rpt./ 4] II Analysis of current major research topics and/or issues facing retail industries. P, RCSC 606.

RCSC 614 -- Theory and Research in Non-Store Retailing (3) II An investigation of retailing that does not involve conventional store facilities including catalog retailing and Internet retailing. The course focuses on theories and concepts that have been used in the investigation of non-store retailing. P, or CR MKTG 400; MKTG 361or B AD 303.

RCSC 624 -- Theory and Research in Services Retailing (3) I Comprehensive investigation of major theoretical perspectives and empirical research on the marketing of profit-centered services to the end consumer. P, MKTG 400, or CR, MKTG 361 or BAD 303.

RCSC 634 -- Current Research in Retail Management (3) I Analysis of research concerning topics related to retail management and planning. Examines theories of institutional change and consumer patronage behavior as well as current research on selected retailer marketing and management strategies. P, or CR, RCSC 400; MKTG 361 or B AD 303.

RCSC 656 -- Consumer Socialization (3) S An analysis of the process by which consumers acquire consumption-related skills, cognition, knowledge, attitudes, and behavior from a life-cycle perspective.

RCSC 676 -- Theories in Retailing and Consumer Behavior (3) I Analysis of major theories and conceptual models guiding research and development of knowledge in the field.
