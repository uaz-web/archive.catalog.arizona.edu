Fall 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Fall 2001; however, some (or all) may not be offered this term.  The course numbers that ARE offered this term link to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).

Italian (ITAL)  Department Info

ITAL 101 -- Elementary Italian I  (4 units)
Description:  Listening, speaking, reading, and writing; introduction to the basic structures and vocabulary of Italian. (Does not count toward the Italian major or minor.)
Course is available:  by special exam for credit only.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring, Summer.

ITAL 102 -- Elementary Italian II  (4 units)
Description:  Listening, speaking, reading and writing; an introduction to the basic structures and vocabulary of Italian, continuation. (Does not count toward the Italian major or minor.)
Prerequisite(s):  ITAL 101 or placement.
Course is available:  by special exam for credit only.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

ITAL 102Z -- Intensive Elementary Italian  (4 units)
Description:  contact department.
Prerequisite(s):  language major or demonstrated language proficiency.
Usually offered:  Fall, Summer.

ITAL 199 -- Independent Study  (1-4 units)
Description:  Qualified students working on an individual basis with professors who have agreed to supervise such work.
May be repeated:  an unlimited number of times.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring, Summer.

ITAL 201 -- Intermediate Italian I  (4 units)
Description:  Continued skill development; reinforcement of basic language skills.
Prerequisite(s):  ITAL 102 or placement.
Course is available:  by special exam for credit only.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

ITAL 202 -- Intermediate Italian II  (4 units)
Description:  Continued skill development; reinforcement of basic language skills.
Prerequisite(s):  ITAL 201 or placement.

Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

ITAL 230A -- Italian Culture: Middle Ages and Renaissance  (3 units)
Description:  Italian culture from the Middle Ages and the Renaissance.
Prerequisite(s):  two courses from Tier One, Traditions and Cultures (TRAD 101,102,103,104).
Approved as:  General Education Tier Two - Humanities.
Usually offered:  Fall.

ITAL 230B -- Italian Culture: Baroque Age to the Present  (3 units)
Description:  Italian Culture: Baroque Age to the Present. Required for the major or minor in Italian Studies option. Taught in English.
Approved as:  General Education Tier Two - Humanities.
Usually offered:  Spring.

ITAL 240 -- Italian Folklore and Popular Culture  (3 units)
Description:  A study of the oral narratives (fairy tales, legends, saints' legends) and customary crafts of ordinary Italian and Italian Americans.
Approved as:  General Education Tier Two - Humanities.
Usually offered:  Fall, Summer.

Description:  Specific methods, objectives, organization of subject matter and evaluation in modern languages.

ITAL 420 -- Italian Civilization  (3 units)
Description:  Historical, geographical, social, and artistic aspects of the development of the culture of Italy. Offered only in Florence, Italy.

Usually offered:  Summer.

ITAL 430 -- Renaissance Studies  (3 units)

ITAL 496A -- Topics in Advanced Italian Studies  (3 units)

Usually offered:  Fall.

ITAL 498 -- Senior Capstone  (1-3 units)
