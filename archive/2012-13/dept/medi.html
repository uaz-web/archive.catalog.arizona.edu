The Department of Medicine is in the College of Medicine.

The department has developed a program designed to introduce medical students to the responsibility of care of the patient.  Together with the staff, students will interview and examine patients, explore what is known of the pathophysiology of the various disorders and observe the effect of appropriate therapeutic modalities.   Students will be assigned patients on the inpatient wards and in the outpatient clinics.  The Department of Medicine includes the following subspecialties all of which have ongoing clinical activity as well as clinical and/or basic science research studies: cardiology, dermatology, endocrinology, gastroenterology, general medicine, geriatrics, hematology/oncology, Indian health services, infectious diseases, integrative medicine, nephrology, pulmonary, and rheumatology.

For more information contact the department listed above.
