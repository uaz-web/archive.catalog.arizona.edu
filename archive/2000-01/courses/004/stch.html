Fall 2000 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses listed below are approved UA courses as of Fall 2000. Not all approved courses will be offered during this term. For course offerings/availability during a specific term, see the Schedule of Classes . Each course number below links to the Schedule of Classes.

Science Teaching (STCH)  College Info

STCH 250 Teaching Science (3) I An introduction to secondary science teaching. Appropriate for students exploring science teaching as a career and required for students in the College of Science teacher preparation program. Includes science classroom observations. P, Sophomore level standing.

STCH 300 Adolescent Development and Learning Science (3) I This course provides an overview of the contemporary theories on student learning in science, adolescent development, and student motivation for learning. The course includes an in-school project with an experienced teacher. P, or CR, STCH 250.

STCH 410 Science Instruction in the Secondary Schools (4) I This course provides secondary science teachers with an understanding of how effective science classrooms are managed to maximize student learning. The course is taught in area secondary schools. P, STCH 250, STCH 300; CR, STCH 420. Consent of the College of Science Teacher Preparation Committee.

STCH 420 Curriculum Decisions and Assessment in Science (4) I A course designed to give secondary science teachers experience making decisions about curriculum and assessing student learning. The course is taught in area secondary schools. P, STCH 250, STCH 300; CR, STCH 410. Consent of the College of Science Teacher Preparation Committee.

STCH 495A Sustaining Partnerships in K-12 Science & Mathematics (1) [Rpt./ 3] I This seminar will help students involved in K-12 science and mathematics education work more effectively with K-12 educators. Students will participate in discussions, practical exercises and problem solving to develop their teaching and communication skills. P, working in a K-12 classroom to improve science, mathematics, engineering or technology. May be convened with STCH 595A.

STCH 595A Sustaining Partnerships in K-12 Science & Mathematics (1) [Rpt./ 3] I For a description of course topics see STCH 495A. Graduate-level requirements include preparation of select presentations, lead discussions in class and online through POLIS. May be convened with STCH 495A.
