MCB 497B -- Science and the Power of the Popular Press (3) II Increasing public understanding of science and technology is vitally important to our country. Many Americans are not aware of the complex ways in which science affects political, economic, and social life. This lack of understanding of roles and ramifications of scientific research and science policy have profound implications for democratic society that is facing decisions about major issues such as how information about the Human Genome Project could affect civil liberties and whether genetically altered crops could affect public health. The purpose of this course is to teach science students how to bridge the gap between scientists and the public through writing well-crafted articles for the popular press.
