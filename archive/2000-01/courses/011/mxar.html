Spring 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

NEW Feature:  Only courses offered in Spring 2001 link to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Spring 2001; however, some may not be offered this term.  Only courses offered this term are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Media Arts (M AR)  Dept Info - College Info

M AR 102 -- Discovering Media (3) II Introductory to media analysis techniques including picture and sound, narration, and representation. Illustrative materials vary with topic. P, open to non-majors only. Approved as Tier Two - Arts.

M AR 199 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] S Contact department for a description of this course.

M AR 200 -- Fundamentals of Theory and Aesthetics in Media Arts (3) I II Survey of the elements which make up video, film, and audio images: light, color, area, depth, movement, and sound in message design and structure. 1R, 1S, 1D.

M AR 201 -- Survey of Media History (4) I II History of film, television and radio; silent and sound film, radio news and drama, international genres and styles, U.S. television.

M AR 203 -- Concepts in New Media (3) II Introduction to the concepts and processes utilized in new media, with examination of social and historical issues relating to emerging technologies. 2R, 2S.

M AR 205 -- Reporting the News (3) I II P, B average in First-Year English composition, consent of department required to enroll. (Identical with JOUR 205, which is home).

M AR 208 -- Law of the Press (3) I II P, first year composition and knowledge of typing. (Identical with JOUR 208, which is home).

M AR 210 -- Beginning Media Production (3) I II Introduction to the fundamental aesthetic and technical aspects of media production, designed to give students experience across a range of digital audio and video equipment and applications. 2R, 2S. Fees. P, M AR 200 or M AR 201.

M AR 221 -- American Cinema: Directors and Genres (3) I Aesthetic and cultural aspects of westerns, comedies, and mysteries; major films by John Ford, Alfred Hitchcock, Howard Hawks, and Blake Edwards. 2R, 2S. P, M AR 200.

M AR 239 -- Speaking in the Arts (3) I II (Identical with T AR 239, which is home).

M AR 241 -- Beginning Photography (3) [Rpt./ 2] I II 2R, 2S. (Identical with ART 241, which is home).

M AR 268R -- Understanding Computer Animation (1) I II Understanding the art of computer animation. May be taken separately or concurrently with M AR 268S. (Identical with ART 268R).

M AR 268S -- Understanding Computer Animation Studio (2) I II Studio designed to accompany M AR 268R. Exploration of, and practice in, computer animations. 8 week course. P, or CR, M AR 268R. (Identical with ART 268S).

M AR 280 -- Introduction to Electronic Journalism (3) I II Survey of the history, organization, and practice of electronic journalism.

M AR 291 -- Preceptorship (1-5) Contact department for a description of this course.

M AR 294 -- Practicum (1-5) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

M AR 297A -- BFA Portfolio Workshop (1) II This course is designed to prepare students with their portfolio materials to be submitted for entry into the Media Arts BFA program. P, M AR 200, M AR 210; 2.5 GPA required.

M AR 297C -- Digital Animation Workshop (3) I II Exploration of, and practice in, computer animation techniques.

M AR 299 -- Independent Study (1-5) [Rpt.] I Contact department for a description of this course.

M AR 299H -- Honors Independent Study (1-5) [Rpt.] II Contact department for a description of this course.

M AR 302 -- Recording Studio Production (3) I II P, consult School of Music and Dance to enroll. (Identical with MUS 302, which is home).

M AR 303 -- Professional Practices (1) Prepares students to meet the professional expectations of media work. Job search strategies (resume writing and interviewing) and professional concepts are studied. P, M AR 101, M AR 200, M AR 304 or M AR 305, and one Writing-Emphasis Course.

M AR 304 -- Beginning Video Production (3) I II Basic principles of video production, including equipment, professional practices, and production techniques. Laboratory experience with camera and editing equipment; exercises and short projects. 2R, 3L. Fees. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 305 -- Introduction to Film Production (3) I II Basic principles of 16mm film production; camera, editing, sound, production techniques and practices; laboratory experience with film production equipment and production of technical exercise films. 3L, 4S. Fees. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 306 -- Digital Animation (3) [Rpt./ 6 units] I Production of computer animation with focus in Character Animation, Modeling/Rigging, or Visual Effects/Rendering. 2R, 2S. P, M AR 268S or ART 268S. (Identical with ART 306).

M AR 308 -- Survey of Media Law and Regulation (3) I II Introduction to the legal and regulatory framework of the electronic media and film: licensing, cross-ownership, public interest, self-regulation, consumer influence, and related topics.

M AR 309 -- History of the Documentary (3) II Major traditions, movements and film makers. Social, aesthetic, and technical aspects of documentary films and videos P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 311A -- Lighting and Composition for Media Production (3) I Practical and theoretical exploration of the function of light and image composition for film and video production. 2R, 2S. P, Advanced Standing in Media Arts.

M AR 312 -- Video Art in America (3) II Investigation of artist-produced video from 1960s to the present. Screenings, critical readings and projects. (Identical with ART 312).

M AR 314 -- Intermediate Video Production (3) I II Intermediate work in video production, with emphasis on digital editing. Producition of exercises and short projects. 2R, 3L. Fees. P, M AR 304. Open to majors and minors only.

M AR 315 -- Intermediate Film Production (3) I II Intermediate work in 16mm synch-sound film production. Students work in teams to produce short synch-sound projects. 2R, 3L. Fees. P, M AR 305. Open to B.F.A. media arts majors and minors only.

M AR 316 -- Radio Production (3) I II Analysis and production of selected radio programs with emphasis on complex radio formats and production techniques. 2R, 3L. P, M AR 304 or M AR 305.

M AR 318 -- Personal Diary Film and Video (3) II Exploration of the history of image making in the home and family context and the integration of film and video making into daily life. Students produce short video assignments. Fees.

M AR 320 -- Media Arts Criticism (3) II Analysis of arguments in journalistic and academic criticism and application of critical approaches in written assignments. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; M AR 200.

M AR 325 -- History of German Cinema (3) I P, two courses from Tier One, Traditions and Cultures (TRAD 101,102,103,104). (Identical with GER 325, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Humanities.

M AR 334 -- Media Industries (3) I Examination of specific topic in media industries: film, broadcasting, cable, recorded music, new media. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 335 -- Topics in Media Studies (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Examination of a specific topic in media studies: film, radio, and/or television. 2R, 2S. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 336 -- History of Japanese Film (3) I II Development of Japanese cinema from its origins through its recognition as a major international art film producer during the 1950s and 1960s. 2R, 2S. P, advanced standing waived for this course. (Identical with JPN 336).

M AR 340 -- Interactive Media Design (3) I II Concepts of interactive design and multimedia production. 3L, 2R. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 349 -- Intermediate Artists' Video (3) I P, M AR 200, M AR 314, acceptance of portfolio. (Identical with ART 349, which is home).

M AR 350 -- Professional Media Interviewing (3) I The interview process and specific interview formats, including survey research, journalistic, and panel formats. Interviewer performance is stressed; practice provided.

M AR 362 -- Writing for Media (3) I II Principles of media writing. Creation of final scripts for radio, television and film presentations. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement. (Identical with JOUR 362).

M AR 364 -- Writing The Narrative (3) I S II Development of scripts for short films and videos. P, Media Arts Advanced Standing.

M AR 371 -- Film/Video Production Financing (3) I II Strategies for production financing for independent film/video projects and ways to position a project in the marketplace. Students will develop a prospectus for their own project. P, M AR 304 or M AR 305.

M AR 372 -- Exhibition Management (3) I II Programming strategies, exhibition techniques, marketing approaches, and management models for film and video series, guest artist presentations, video installations, conferences, and festivals. 2R, 3L.

M AR 376 -- Media Market Research (3) I Interpretation and utilization of ratings, surveys, polls and other quantitative and qualitative measures of the attitudes, opinions and behaviors of media audiences, relationships to social and industrial concerns.

M AR 380 -- Writing for News and Documentary (3) I Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; M AR advanced standing. (Identical with JOUR 380, which is home).

M AR 397D -- Computer Imaging (3) I Production workshop in which students develop and complete computer imaging projects. P, M AR 306, M AR 406.

M AR 401 -- Advanced Pre-Production (3) I II Development of scripts or proposals, completion of pre-production for capstone projects. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 406 -- Multimedia (3) I Principles and processes of multimedia assembly with an emphasis on interactive skills. 2R, 2S. P, M AR advanced standing. May be convened with M AR 506.

M AR 419A -- Media Arts and Business: Film (3) II Examination of the business processes and economic dynamics of the US motion picture industries. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only. P, advanced standing in Media Arts.

M AR 421 -- Cultural Theory and Criticism of Media (3) I Critical and cultural theories and their application to media arts, including mass culture, empiricism, technoculture, political economy. P, M AR 200, M AR 320. May be convened with M AR 521.

M AR 423 -- Representation of Gender in the Media (3) I Investigation of gender as a social and cultural construct through the critical analysis of media products including television, film, and advertisements. P, M AR 200, M AR 320. (Identical with W S 423). May be convened with M AR 523.

M AR 424 -- Theory and Criticism: Film, TV and New Media (3) I Examination of major theories of film, television, and new media; application to the contemporary media arts.

M AR 426 -- Sexuality in Media Narratives (3) I Analysis of sexual representation in popular and underground film, music video and avant-garde video art. May be convened with M AR 526.

M AR 427 -- Feminist Media Theory (3) II Includes psychoanalysis, semiotics, materialism, race and class analysis, and feminist media production. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 431 -- Commercial Intertextuality (3) II Analysis of industrial constraints on the production and circulation of media texts with emphasis on intertextual references. P, M AR advanced standing.

M AR 434 -- Media Industries (3) I Examination of a specific topic in media industries: ownership and concentration; media markets and industrial integration. P, M AR advanced standing. (Identical with MAP 434). May be convened with M AR 534.

M AR 435 -- Hollywood Film and Television (3) [Rpt./ 1] I Historical examination of Hollywood-produced film and television: styles and genres, industrial processes, social context and impact. May be convened with M AR 535.

M AR 437 -- Ethnographic Film and Video (3) I II Survey of ethnographic film and video from 1895 to present. Examines representative films and tapes in terms of media and anthropological theories P, for media arts majors: M AR advanced standing. For anthropology majors: junior standing. (Identical with ANTH 437).

M AR 442 -- Interactive Storytelling (3) II Theory and production of storytelling on the Web; aesthetics and design, navigation and interactivity, creative approaches to web-based narrative. This course is temporary and will be offered during Spring 2001 only. 3L, 2R.

M AR 449 -- Advanced Artists' Video (3) [Rpt./ 1] II P, ART 349 or M AR 314; acceptance of portfolio. (Identical with ART 449, which is home).

M AR 450 -- Conducting Media Campaigns (3) II Analysis of the development and distribution of information through the media. Press releases, fact sheets, public service announcements, interviews, press conferences, and public hearings are studied.

M AR 452 -- Latinos/as in Hollywood: Identity Politics and the Politics of Representation (3) I Examines the representation of Latinos/as in Hollywood film and television from early cinema to contemporary products. Emphasis on relationship between these representations and the shifting patterns of Latino/a identity in the U.S. 2R, 2S.

M AR 468 -- Visual Storytelling (3) S Principles of story development through storyboard and storyreel creation. P, advanced standing in Media Arts or equivalent in Art. (Identical with ART 468).

M AR 470 -- The Press and Society (3) I II (Identical with JOUR 470, which is home).

M AR 472 -- Broadcast and Cable Management (3) I II Investigation of media management techniques. Scheduling, organizational structure, networks and affiliates, ethics, legal constraints, syndication, personnel and related topics.

M AR 473 -- Media Management Practice (3) Modern media management issues. Students will read current literature, lead discussions, write analyses, and complete a final project. Writing and speaking emphasized.

M AR 476 -- Broadcast and Cable Programming (3) I Investigation of principles, techniques, and current issues in programming for radio and television stations (commercial and public) and cable systems. May be convened with M AR 576.

M AR 478 -- Creative Media Advertising (3) I II Study of and practice in the creative aspects of advertising, including idea origination, media selection, and construction and evaluation of projects.

M AR 479 -- International Film and TV Business (3) II This course examines the practices of the international film and television business; the global organization of the entertainment industry; producing film and television for national and global audiences; social, aesthetic and economic implications of global media.

M AR 480 -- Reporting for Broadcast News (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II 2R, 3L. P, M AR 304, M AR 308, M AR advanced standing. (Identical with JOUR 480, which is home).
