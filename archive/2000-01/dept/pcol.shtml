PHCL Course Descriptions: Pharmacology
PCOL Course Descriptions: Pharmacology and Toxicology

The Graduate Interdisciplinary Program in Pharmacology and Toxicology is administered by the College of Pharmacy.  The Department of Pharmacology is in the College of Medicine.

Degrees, Majors, Minors and Options:

Graduate Degrees -- consult the Graduate Catalog or program office for details.

  • Master of Science (M.S.)
  • Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.)

Major and Degrees

  • Pharmacology and Toxicology (M.S., Ph.D.)

The graduate program in pharmacology and toxicology is interdisciplinary, with a faculty of 51, offering programs leading to the Master of Science and Doctor of Philosophy degrees in pharmacology and toxicology.  The Master of Science degree also offers a special concentration in Perfusion Science or Industrial Hygiene.  Faculty members have research interests in carcinogenesis/cancer chemotherapy, cardiovascular pharmacology, clinical pharmacology, endocrine pharmacology, environmental and biochemical toxicology, immunopharmacology, molecular/biochemical pharmacology, molecular toxicology, neuropharmacology, natural products chemistry, pharmacokinetics, and toxicology.

For more information contact the program office listed above.
