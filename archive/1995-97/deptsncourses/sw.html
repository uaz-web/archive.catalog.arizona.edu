The Department of Soil and Water Science provides students with a variety of programs in environmental science, soil science, water quality and soil-plant-nutrient-water relationships. The department offers the Bachelor of Science degree with a major in environmental science, and the Bachelor of Science in Agriculture degree with a major in soil and water science. Graduate Master of Science and Doctor of Philosophy degrees with a major in soil and water science also are available.

A major in environmental science must satisfy the general education requirements for any college at the University of Arizona and complete the following core courses: ABE 220; AREC 217; CHEM 103a, 103b, 104a, 104b, 241a, 322, 323; ECOL 181; MATH 125a; OSH 486; PHYS 102a; STAT 263; SW 200, 201, 305, 325, 393, 444; TOX 302X; and three units from HWR 415, POL 481 or RNR 480. In addition, the requirements for one of the following focal areas must be met by selecting 21 units in the focal area. 1)Land and water This focus is primarily on pollution relating to land and water. It includes hazardous waste, landfills, surface and groundwater, and processes in the vadose zone. 2)Environmental microbiology This focus primarily addresses the remediation of contaminated sites, using microbiological methods. 3)Environmental engineering science This focus is a hybrid of science and engineering, developed cooperatively with the Department of Chemical and Environmental Engineering. Remaining classes may be taken as electives. A total of 130 credits are needed for graduation.

A major in soil and water science must satisfy the general education requirements of the College of Agriculture and must complete the following courses: CHEM 103a-103b, 104a-104b, 241a or 322, 323, PHYS 102a, 180a, MCB 181, GEOS 101 or 110, and MATH 123. The University Writing-Emphasis Course requirements can be met by completion of SW 411, 426, 450, 461 or 470.

A major with a focus in soil science emphasizes the physical, biological and chemical interactions in the soil environment. Course requirements are SW 200, 201, 325, 411, 431 and two of the following: SW 450, 453, 470. HWR 250, SW 105, 106 are recommended but not required for majors with a focus in soil science.

A major with a focus in soil-plant-nutrient-water relationships must complete SW 200, 201, 316, 317, 431, ABE 250 or 404, SW 411, 470.

Students are encouraged to take additional classes in chemistry, physics, mathematics, biology and geosciences, as well as classes in computer science and statistics.

A minor in soil and water science, focusing on either soil-plant-nutrient-water relationships or soil science, is available to students from other disciplines. Requirements include SW 200, 201 and three of the following: 316 and 317, 411, 425, 431 and 470. In addition, students must take six units (at least three upper-division units) of geosciences, hydrology, irrigation or soil and water science.

For the B.S. degree in Agriculture with a major in microbiology, the MIC crosslisted classes in this department satisfy the 13-unit College of Agriculture requirement.
