Degrees, Majors, Minors and Options

The graduate program in pharmacology and toxicology is interdisciplinary, with a faculty1997 of 49, offering programs leading to the Doctor of Philosophy/Master of Science degree in pharmacology and toxicology. The Master of Science degree also offers a special concentration in Perfusion Science or Industrial Hygiene. Faculty members have research interests in carcinogenesis/cancer chemotherapy, cardiovascular pharmacology, clinical pharmacology, endocrine pharmacology, environmental and biochemical toxicology, immunopharmacology, molecular/biochemical pharmacology, molecular toxicology, neuropharmacology, and pharmacokinetics.

For academic requirements for graduate degrees, consult the Graduate Catalog and the departmental office listed above.
