Winter 06/Spring 07 Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Winter 2006 and Spring 2007; however, some (or all) may not be offered in either term.  The courses that are offered in Spring link to the Schedule of Classes.  Classes with alternative delivery modes (Web based, cable TV, correspondence, etc) are noted in the Schedule at the section level.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).  For explanations of course elements see the Key to Course Descriptions.

NOTE: When registering for an UA South course please be sure to note the location of the course offering.

Language and Culture in Education (LCEV)  Department Info - Offered by UA South

LCEV 402 -- Home School Community Connection  (3 units)
Description:  Analysis of issues in the home, school and community connection for ELL students. This course will cover issues that affect ELL students directly, such as parent involvement, intercultural aspects of the home, school, community connection. It will investigate cultural and socio-linguistic aspects of education and how they affect success in school.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the University of Arizona South education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 502.
Usually offered:  Fall.

LCEV 404 -- Cultural Diversity in Education  (3 units)
Description:  Introduction to aspects of culture that affect education. An overview of different cultures will serve as the basis for discussing issues pertaining to school achievement.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
May be convened with:  LCEV 504.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

LCEV 408 -- Methods of Teaching English to English Language Learners  (3 units)
Description:  Methodology for the teaching of English to the English Language Learner (ELL). This course will familiarize the students with a historical overview of different methods as well as influences from the field of psychology. It will introduce students to what is currently considered quality methodology in ELL education.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the University of Arizona South education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 508.
Usually offered:  Fall.

LCEV 410 -- Foundations of TESOL  (3 units)
Description:  This course will address first and second language acquisition theories. It will review seminal research as well as instructional applications for ELL students.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the University of Arizona South education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 510.
Usually offered:  Fall.

LCEV 414 -- Literacy Acquisition for ELL (English Language Learners) Students  (3 units)
Description:  Analysis of reading and writing situations encountered by ELL students; phonological, semantic, and syntactic aspects of instruction; methods and materials.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
May be convened with:  LCEV 514.
Usually offered:  Spring.

LCEV 418 -- Bilingual Methods  (3 units)
Description:  This course analyses and evaluates methods and materials used in bilingual education programs, effective strategies in first and second languages, concurrent and separate language approaches and cooperative models.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the UAS education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 518.
Usually offered:  Spring.

LCEV 428 -- Bilingual Curriculum  (3 units)
Description:  Theory and application of curriculum development to bilingual instructional programs: designs, organizational patterns, materials and media, change strategies, and evaluation. Graduate-level requirements include a ten article annotated bibliography on topics related to the course.
Grading:  Regular grades are awarded for this course: A B C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the UAS education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 528.
Usually offered:  Summer.

LCEV 494A -- ELL (English Language Learners) Practicum  (3 units)
Description:  A reflective analysis and application, on an individual basis, of previously studied theory and practice, in the teaching of ELL students.
Grading:  Alternative grades are awarded for this course: S P C D E.
Prerequisite(s):  Students must have been accepted into the UAS education program or already have a teaching certificate.
May be convened with:  LCEV 594A.
Usually offered:  Spring.
