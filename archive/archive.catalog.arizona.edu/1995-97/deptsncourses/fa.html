The following courses in Fine Arts are interdepartmental in subject matter and in instruction. Therefore, they are offered by Fine Arts rather than by a specific department.

207. Western Civilization and the Arts: The Twentieth Century (3) I The arts as an interdisciplinary framework of human heritage from which connections are made to contemporary issues in ethics, philosophy, science, law, and politics.

307. Western Civilization and the Arts: Paleolithic Through Renaissance (3) II The arts as an interdisciplinary framework of human heritage from which connections are made to historical issues in ethics, philosophy, science, law, and politics.

317.Western Civilization and the Arts: Baroque Through Nineteenth Century (3) II The arts as an interdisciplinary framework of human heritage from which connections are made to historical issues in ethics, philosophy, science, law, and politics.
