Academic Policies

Accelerated Master’s Program (AMP)

The information on this Catalog page has been superseded by information maintained on the Academic Affairs-Academic Programs web site. Please refer to most up-to-date version of this page for links to the appropriate pages at the Academic Affairs-Academic Programs site.

Program Overview

The Graduate College offers exceptional undergraduate students in selected majors the opportunity to earn both a bachelor’s degree and master’s degree in as few as 5 years. The Accelerated Master’s Program (AMP) is for the top undergraduates in participating majors who will continue in a graduate program in the same, or closely related, UA discipline. To determine if this in an option in your major, see the list of currently approved AMPs.

Admission Requirements for the AMP

Interested students should check with the department offering the AMP regarding additional or more stringent requirements.

At minimum the applicant must:

  1. Have completed a minimum of 75 undergraduate credits; a minimum of 90 undergraduate credits must be completed at the time of entry to the AMP.

  2. Have a minimum 3.300 GPA.

  3. Have completed at least 12 undergraduate units of University Credit (in residence at the UA main campus).
  4. Have completed or almost completed UA General Education Requirements.
  5. Expect to complete the bachelor’s degree within 4 years.
  6. Submit a Graduate College application with application fee.
  7. Demonstrate the maturity necessary for success in an accelerated, highly competitive program.

Degree and Tuition Policies

  1. During the first 3 years of their academic career at the UA (while earning approximately 90 credits), students take undergraduate courses and pay undergraduate tuition.

  2. After admission to an AMP, during the fourth year when students have senior status, they may take up to 12 units of graduate courses that apply toward both the bachelor’s and master’s degrees. During that year, students continue paying undergraduate tuition and remain eligible for undergraduate scholarships.

  3. After completion of all bachelor’s degree requirements (minimum of 120 credits for most degree programs), students who have been admitted to an AMP will have graduate status, pay graduate tuition, and be eligible for graduate assistantships.

  4. Note: Should a student in an AMP have completed 12 graduate credits but not yet completed the bachelor’s degree, the student will have graduate status for financial aid and tuition purposes. The student will not be eligible for undergraduate scholarships, nor for graduate assistantships.

  5. At least 12 graduate credits must be taken while in graduate status and after earning the bachelor’s degree.

  6. Students should complete their bachelor’s degree as soon as possible, but no later than one semester before earning their master’s degree. Please direct specific questions about AMPs to an academic advisor serving the discipline offering an AMP.
