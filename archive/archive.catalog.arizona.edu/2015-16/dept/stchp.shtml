The Science Teacher Preparation Program is in the College of Science.  The baccalaureate program provides science majors the opportunity to take courses and engage in classroom experiences that will lead to a Bachelor of Science degree in Science Education, to eligibility for secondary science teacher certification, or to a minor in Science Education.

Alternatively, science majors may graduate in their disciplinary majors and simultaneously complete the Teacher Preparation Program courses to become eligible for teacher certification. This provides a degree of flexibility not common among other teacher preparation programs. 

The M.S. program with a major in Natural Science for Teachers is designed for middle and high school teachers who seek advanced, specialized training in the biological sciences to increase their knowledge of the discipline and enhance their classroom teaching methods.
