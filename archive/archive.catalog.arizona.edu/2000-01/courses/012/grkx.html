Summer 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses offered in Summer 2001 are linked to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Summer 2001; however, some may not be offered this Summer.  The courses that ARE offered this Summer, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Greek (GRK )  Dept Info - College Info

GRK 101 -- Elementary Classical Greek I (4) I II Introduction to ancient Greek for students of the Bible and of the classical authors.

GRK 102 -- Elementary Classical Greek II (4) I II Second semester Greek. P, GRK 101.

GRK 103 -- Elementary Modern Greek I (4) I Development of skills in conversation, composition, and reading with emphasis upon audiovisual practice.

GRK 104 -- Elementary Modern Greek II (4) II Second semester modern Greek. P, GRK 103.

GRK 199 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 201 -- Intermediate Classical Greek I (4) I Selections from classical Greek chosen in accordance with the student's needs and interest. P, GRK 102.

GRK 202 -- Intermediate Classical Greek II (4) II Selections from classical Greek. P, GRK 201.

GRK 203 -- Intermediate Modern Greek I (4) I Pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary of modern Greek; development of skills in conversation, composition, and reading; emphasis on aural-oral skills. P, GRK 104.

GRK 204 -- Intermediate Modern Greek II (4) II Conversation, composition, and reading. P, GRK 203.

GRK 299 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 391H -- Honors Preceptorship (1-3) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 399 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 399H -- Honors Independent Study (1-3) Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 402 -- Greek Reading Course (3) [Rpt./ 6 units] I II Readings in major Greek authors including Homer, Plato, and the historians and dramatists. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 502.

GRK 412 -- Readings in Greek Philosophy (3) [Rpt./ 6 units] I II Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. (Identical with PHIL 412, which is home). May be convened with GRK 512.

GRK 421 -- Greek Lyric Poetry (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Study in Greek of the early Greek Lyric writers from Archilochus to Bacchylides, including Pindar. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 521.

GRK 422 -- Readings in Greek Drama (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Close reading in Greek of either (1) tragedy-one play each by Aeschylus, Sophocles and Euripides or (2) comedy-two plays of Aristophanes, one of Menander. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 522.

GRK 424 -- Homer (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Close reading of selections from the Iliad and Odyssey in Greek and an introduction to the critical secondary literature. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 524.

GRK 430 -- Readings in Greek Historians (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Selections from Herodotus and Thucydides with an introduction to the critical literature. Readings in Greek. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 530.

GRK 431 -- Greek Orators (3) I II Readings in Greek from Lysias, Isocrates and Demosthenes as sources for ancient rhetoric, politics, and private life. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 531.

GRK 432 -- Literature of Archaic Greece (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Readings in Greek from Hesiod and the early lyric poets. The agricultural perspective and the anti-heroic ideal. Writing Emphasis Course. P, satisfaction of the upper-division writing proficiency requirement; GRK 202. May be convened with GRK 532.

GRK 498 -- Senior Capstone (1-3) I II Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 498H -- Honors Thesis (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 499 -- Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt.] Contact department for a description of this course.

GRK 502 -- Greek Reading Course (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 402. Graduate-level requirements include extensive reading and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 402.

GRK 512 -- Readings in Greek Philosophy (3) [Rpt./ 6 units] I II P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. (Identical with PHIL 512, which is home). May be convened with GRK 412.

GRK 521 -- Greek Lyric Poetry (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 421. Graduate-level requirements include extensive reading and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 421.

GRK 522 -- Readings in Greek Drama (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 422. Graduate-level requirements include extensive reading and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 422.

GRK 524 -- Homer (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 424. Graduate-level requirements include extensive reading and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 424.

GRK 530 -- Readings in Greek Historians (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 430. Graduate-level requirements include extensive readings and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 430.

GRK 531 -- Greek Orators (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II For a description of course topics see GRK 431. Graduate-level requirements include extensive readings and an in-depth paper. P, 3 units of 400-level Greek. May be convened with GRK 431.
