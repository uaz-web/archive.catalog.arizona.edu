Summer 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses offered in Summer 2001 are linked to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Summer 2001; however, some may not be offered this Summer.  The courses that ARE offered this Summer, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Pharmacology (PHCL)  Dept Info - College Info

For descriptions of medical student electives, see the College of Medicine Electives Manual.

PHCL 800 -- Research (1-6) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

PHCL 801 -- The Pharmacological Basis of Therapeutics (6) [Rpt.] I Actions of chemical agents upon living material at all levels of organization, with emphasis on mechanisms of action of prototype drugs; foundation for a rational approach to human therapeutics and toxicology. P, PSIO 580 or PSIO 601; and course equivalent to BIOC 462A. Available as both PHCL 801 and PCOL 501A/B.

PHCL 815A -- Clinical Pharmacology (3-6) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

PHCL 825 -- Human Neuroscience (6) I II P, consent of instructor. (Identical with MED 825, which is home).

PHCL 891A -- Pharmacology (3-12) [Rpt./ 12 units] I II Contact department for a description of this course.

PHCL 891L -- Perfusion Science (1-3) [Rpt.] Students register for 3 units Fall and Spring semesters and one unit Summer Session I and II. (Identical with SURG 891L).

PHCL 899 -- Independent Study (3-12) [Rpt.] I II Contact department for a description of this course.
