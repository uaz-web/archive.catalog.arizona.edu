Summer 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses offered in Summer 2001 are linked to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Summer 2001; however, some may not be offered this Summer.  The courses that ARE offered this Summer, in either Pre-Session, Summer I or Summer II, are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Military Aerospace Studies (ML A)  Dept Info - College Info

ML A 100A -- Foundations of the Air Force (2) I First Year GMC* Survey of the doctrine, mission, and organization of the U.S.A.F.; U.S. strategic offensive and defensive forces; U.S. general purpose and aerospace support forces. P, ML A 100A is not prerequisite to ML A 100B.

ML A 100B -- Foundations of the Air Force (2) II First Year GMC* Survey of the doctrine, mission, and organization of the U.S.A.F.; U.S. strategic offensive and defensive forces; U.S. general purpose and aerospace support forces. P, ML A 100A is not prerequisite to ML A 100B.

ML A 110 -- Introductory Flight School (2) [Rpt./ 1] I II Introduction to the main fields of aircraft and airport operations. Topics included are basic aerodynamics, FAA regulations, radio procedures, human factors, cockpit management, meteorology, and interpretation of weather data and navigation.

ML A 200A -- Airpower History (2) I Second Year GMC* Survey of the development of aviation from the advent of the air age to the present, with emphasis on military aviation and its relationship with political and economic aspects of historical world situations. P, ML A 200A is not prerequisite to ML A 200B.

ML A 200B -- Airpower History (2) II Second Year GMC* Survey of the development of aviation from the advent of the air age to the present, with emphasis on military aviation and its relationship with political and economic aspects of historical world situations. P, ML A 200A is not prerequisite to ML A 200B.

ML A 300A -- Leadership Studies (3) I Theory and application of leadership and management, with emphasis on human relations, motivation, communication for managers, organizational behavior and Total Quality Management (TQM) principles and processes. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 300B -- Leadership Studies (3) II Theory and application of leadership and management, with emphasis on human relations, motivation, communication for managers, organizational behavior and Total Quality Management (TQM) principles and processes. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 400A -- National Security Affairs (3) I Critical analysis of various aspects of the military in American society and its application and effects on the world political and economic environment. P, consult department before enrolling.

ML A 400B -- National Security Affairs (3) II Critical analysis of various aspects of the military in American society and its application and effects on the world political and economic environment. P, consult department before enrolling.
