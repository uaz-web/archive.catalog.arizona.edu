Fall 2000 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

Courses listed below are approved UA courses as of Fall 2000. Not all approved courses will be offered during this term. For course offerings/availability during a specific term, see the Schedule of Classes . Each course number below links to the Schedule of Classes.

Latin (LAT )  Dept Info - College Info

LAT 101 Elementary Latin I (4) I II An introduction to the basic grammar, syntax and vocabulary of Latin through reading and composition.

LAT 102 Elementary Latin II (4) I II Second semester Latin. P, LAT 101.

LAT 112 Accelerated Latin I (6) S Equivalent of LAT 101 and 102. Covers all basic grammar and syntax. P, previous language experience or consult department before enrolling. Credit allowed for only one of LAT 112, LAT 101, LAT 102.

LAT 199 Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt./]

LAT 201 Intermediate Latin I (4) I Review of Latin grammar with readings from Cicero and other prose writers. P, LAT 102.

LAT 202 Intermediate Latin II (4) II Readings from Ovid, Virgil and other Latin poets. P, LAT 201.

LAT 212 Accelerated Latin II (6) S Equivalent of LAT 201 and 202. Reading and composition, prose and poetry. P, LAT 102 or LAT 112; LAT 101.

LAT 299 Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt./]

LAT 399 Independent Study (1-3) [Rpt./]

LAT 400 Prose of the Roman Republic (3) [Rpt./ 1] I Extended readings from Sallust, Cicero and Caesar with some grammatical review; development of skills in rapid readings and sight reading. P, LAT 202.

LAT 401 Latin Reading Course (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Readings in one of the following: epic, lyric, drama, history, oratory, satire, epistles, novel, philosophical, technical or medieval literature. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 501.

LAT 403 Late Antique Literature (3) [Rpt./ 1] Selections from genres and/or authors, both Christian and non-Christian, from the late antique period. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 503.

LAT 405 Latin Composition (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Analysis of Latin prose style, review of Latin grammar, practice in composing Latin prose. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 505.

LAT 413 Augustan Literature (3) [Rpt./ 1] II Readings from a major writer or writers of the Augustan age. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 513.

LAT 414 Medieval Latin (3) I II Survey of Latin literature during the thousand years between the end of the classical period and the beginning of the Renaissance. Readings in Latin. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 514.

LAT 415 Latin Love Elegy (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Reading in the Latin texts of Ovid, Tibullus and Propertius. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 515.

LAT 420 Latin Paleography (3) Identification and reading of major Latin bookhands of the Middle Ages and the Renaissance. Problems in text transmission, corruptions and emendation. P, 3 units of Latin at the 400 level. May be convened with LAT 520.

LAT 425 Cicero (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II The life of Cicero illustrated by means of close reading of selected works in Latin. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 525.

LAT 426 Roman Historians (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Readings in Latin from the Roman historians and biographers. May be repeated without duplication of readings. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 526.

LAT 428 Silver Age Latin (3) [Rpt./ 1] I II Readings from Latin writers of the early Empire. Readings will be in Latin. Writing Emphasis Course. P, LAT 400. May be convened with LAT 528.

LAT 486 Issues in Latin Teaching Methodology (3) I Introduction to methodologies, pedagogical skills and teaching strategies, texts and resources essential to Latin language instruction. Readings selected from modern educational theorists as well as ancient authors. P, LAT 400. Open to majors only. May be convened with LAT 586.
