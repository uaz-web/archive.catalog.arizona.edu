WS M 406 -- Applied Hydraulics (3) I GRD P, MATH 111 or MATH 113 or MATH 125; PHYS 102. Open to non-majors only. (Identical with ABE 406, which is home). May be convened with WS M 506.

WS M 408 -- Wildland Fire Management (3) I Principles of fire behavior in forest, range and other vegetation types; interrelationships of fuels, weather, and topography; pyrolysis and combustion processes; effects of fire; fuels inventory; prevention, detection, and control techniques; fire danger rating and fire behavior modeling. May be convened with WS M 508.

WS M 426 -- Soil and Water Conservation Engineering (3) II S 1.5 ES, 1.5 ED. P, ABE 406 or C E 321 or A ME 331. (Identical with ABE 426, which is home). May be convened with WS M 526.

WS M 435 -- Water Management in Dryland Ecosystems (3) I Hydrologic principles as applied to arid and semi-arid ecosystems with water management applications in dryland resources management. P, MATH 160 or MATH 263; SWES 201. May be convened with WS M 535.

WS M 460 -- Watershed Hydrology (3) I Application of fundamental principles to quantifying the basic hydrologic processes occurring on watersheds. (Identical with HWR 460). May be convened with WS M 560.

WS M 462 -- Watershed Management (3) II Evaluating hydrologic impacts of management activities on watersheds to include silviculture, range, mining, and recreation use. 2R, 3L. P, WS M 460 or one course in hydrology. May be convened with WS M 562.

WS M 463 -- Plant-Water Relations (3) II P, PL S 360. (Identical with PL S 463, which is home). May be convened with WS M 563.

WS M 464 -- Introduction to Dendrochronology (4) I (Identical with GEOS 464, which is home). May be convened with WS M 564.

WS M 465 -- Erosion and Environment (3) I Principles of erosion and sediment yield by water and wind; modeling and measurement of soil erosion; strategies for erosion and sediment control. Field Trips. P, WS M 460 or equivalent. May be convened with WS M 565.

WS M 467 -- Advanced Watershed Hydrology (3) II Advanced topics in watershed hydrology; rainfall-runoff, infiltration, overland flow routing, sediment modeling, statistical analysis and research methods in hydrology. P, WS M 460. (Identical with HWR 467, ABE 467). May be convened with WS M 567.

WS M 468 -- Wildland Water Quality (3) II Introduction to water quality and its influences in natural environments. Interactions with land management and relationships to the larger issues of environmental quality. May be convened with WS M 568.

WS M 515 -- Mensuration (3) II Measurement and inventory of forest land, forest growth, raw materials and products. Special analysis of mensurational data from an arid land inventory. P, RNR 271, RNR 321, MATH 113.

WS M 535 -- Water Management in Dryland Ecosystems (3) I For a description of course topics see WS M 435. Graduate-level requirements include a report and oral presentation on a topic related to hydrology or water management in dryland ecosystems. (Identical with ARL 535). May be convened with WS M 435.

WS M 560 -- Watershed Hydrology (3) I For a description of course topics see WS M 460. Contact department for a description of this course. (Identical with HWR 560). May be convened with WS M 460.

WS M 562 -- Watershed Management (3) II For a description of course topics see WS M 462. Graduate-level requirements include an in-depth paper on the application of hydrologic principles to problems in watershed management. 2R, 3L. May be convened with WS M 462.

WS M 563 -- Plant-Water Relations (3) II (Identical with PL S 563, which is home). May be convened with WS M 463.

WS M 564 -- Introduction to Dendrochronology (4) I (Identical with GEOS 564, which is home). May be convened with WS M 464.

WS M 565 -- Erosion and Environment (3) I For a description of course topics see WS M 465. Graduate-level requirements include a research paper and oral presentation. May be convened with WS M 465.

WS M 567 -- Advanced Watershed Hydrology (3) I For a description of course topics see WS M 467. Graduate students will be required to do additional exercises. (Identical with ABE 567, HWR 567). May be convened with WS M 467.

WS M 568 -- Wildland Water Quality (3) II For a description of course topics see WS M 468. Graduate-level requirements include a class report and presentation on a negotiated topic of interest. May be convened with WS M 468.

WS M 569 -- Spatial Analysis of Hydrology and Watershed Management (3) Geographic information systems (GIS) as a tool for hydrologists and environmental managers. Topics relate to the application of GIS including classification and suitability analysis, interpolation techniques, terrain analysis, model integration, and visualization. Examines sources of potential error and the ramifications. 2R, 3L. P, RNR 417 or RNR 517. (Identical with HWR 569).
