Spring 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

NEW Feature:  Only courses offered in Spring 2001 link to the Schedule of Classes.

All courses listed below are approved to be taught as of  Spring 2001; however, some may not be offered this term.  Only courses offered this term are linked to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about future offerings).

Engineering and Mines (ENGR)  College Info

ENGR 102 -- Introduction to Engineering (3) Engineering design, effective team participation and career preparation. Students are expected to participate in hands-on design projects, develop education/career plans and initiate development of the personal and management skills necessary for life long learning. 3ED. P, or CR, MATH 124 or MATH 125.

ENGR 170 -- Problem Solving Using Computers (3) The design of problem-solving algorithms, their implementation in a structured programming language, and their application in engineering.

ENGR 195B -- Materials Science and Engineering (1) I II (Identical with MSE 195B, which is home).

ENGR 195C -- Women in Science and Engineering (1) I Contact department for a description of this course. (Identical with W S 195C).

ENGR 196A -- Survey of Engineering Professions (1) I Contact department for a description of this course.

ENGR 196B -- Planning Engineering Design (1) I Contact department for a description of this course.

ENGR 196H -- Honors Proseminar (1) II Contact department for a description of this course.

ENGR 249 -- Technology and The Growth of Civilizations (3) II (Identical with ANTH 249, which is home).

ENGR 251 -- Social Constraints on Engineering (3) I (Identical with MSE 251, which is home).

ENGR 257 -- Materials Science of Art and Archaeological Objects (3) II 3ES. P, two courses from Tier One, Natural Sciences (NATS 101, 102, 104). Credit for this course or MSE 259 but not for both. (Identical with MSE 257, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Natural Sciences.

ENGR 258 -- Laboratory for Materials Science of Art and Archaeological Objects (1) II 1ES. P, two courses from Tier One, Natural Sciences (NATS 101, 102, 104). (Identical with MSE 258, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Natural Sciences.

ENGR 259 -- Society and the Technology of Art and Archaeological Objects (3) II P, two courses from Tier One, Individuals and Societies (INDV 101, 102, 103, 104). Credit is allowed for this course or MSE 257, but not for both. (Identical with MSE 259, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Individuals and Societies.

ENGR 265 -- Engineering Economic Analysis (3) 3ES. P, MATH 129, ENGR 102. (Identical with SIE 265, which is home).

ENGR 317 -- Science Fiction Studies (3) II P, two courses from Tier One, Traditions and Cultures (TRAD 101,102,103,104). (Identical with MSE 317, which is home). Approved as Tier Two - Humanities.

ENGR 320 -- Introduction to Computer Aided Design (3) II 1R, 6L. 1ED, 2ES. P, computer literacy (Windows/UNIX). (Identical with ABE 320, which is home).

ENGR 435 -- Corrosion and Degradation (3) II 2ES, 1ED. P, MSE 331R or MSE 412; or, P or CR, CHEM 480B. (Identical with MSE 435, which is home).

ENGR 454 -- Law for Engineers and Scientists (3) II (Identical with CHEE 454, which is home). May be convened with ENGR 554.

ENGR 479 -- Culture and Materials Technology (3) I (Identical with ANTH 479, which is home).

ENGR 486 -- Technology and Society (3) I 1ES, 2ED. (Identical with MSE 486, which is home).

ENGR 488 -- Scanning Electron Microscopy (3) I 2R, 3L. 3ES. P, consult department before enrolling. (Identical with MSE 488, which is home).

ENGR 498 -- Senior Capstone (1-3) I II Contact department for a description of this course.

ENGR 501 -- Planning for Discovery (3) [Rpt./ 1] II P, a basic course in physical chemistry. (Identical with MSE 501, which is home).

ENGR 502 -- Research Proposal Preparation (3) [Rpt./ 1] I (Identical with MSE 502, which is home).

ENGR 554 -- Law for Engineers/Scientists (3) II (Identical with CHEE 554, which is home). May be convened with ENGR 454.

ENGR 696A -- Science and Social Theory (3) II Contact department for a description of this course. (Identical with SOC 696A).
