College of Humanities

Undergraduate Minors Policy

A minor is required by most B.A. programs in the College of Humanities. Exceptions are made for the interdisciplinary studies major and any double major. Transfer students may discuss with the major advisor use of prior course work for the minor. Many degree programs require a specific structured minor. For information about whether or not your degree program requires a specific minor, contact your major advisor or view the APRR for your degree program.

Some major programs permit a split minor (a minimum of 8 units in one department and 12 units in another department) or a thematic minor. The thematic minor is developed around a theme identified by the student, using courses from three or more disciplines. A thematic minor form, available at the Office of Academic Services, must be submitted at the time of application for degree certification.

Requirements for a humanities minor:

  • at least 20 units, and
  • unless otherwise noted in the requirements listed for a specific minor, at least 9 of the 20 units must be upper-division University Credit*
  • The following courses may not be used as minor courses: first-year composition, courses below Math 124, military aerospace studies, military or naval science, activity courses in exercise and sports science, first-year language courses that are also used to satisfy the language requirement

Most departments who offer minors suggest core course to fulfill minor requirements. Contact the minor department for more information.

*Some humanities minors do not require a minimum of 9 upper-division units. Requirements for these minors are defined by the minor department. Contact your academic advisor for more information.
