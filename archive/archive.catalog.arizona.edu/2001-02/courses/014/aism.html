Fall 2001 Course Descriptions
Key to Course Descriptions

All courses below are approved to be taught in Fall 2001; however, some (or all) may not be offered this term.  The course numbers that ARE offered this term link to the Schedule of Classes.  The complete list below is a good indicator of what may be offered over the next few years (contact department about offerings).

Arizona International College: Natural Science and Mathematics (AISM)  Department Info

AISM 100 -- Mathematics in Life, Society and the World  (3 units)
Description:  This course is an introductory survey of a variety of topics such as the history of mathematics and mathematics in art, music, and nature. Basic mathematical concepts such as critical thinking, logic, algebra, voting systems, population growth and radioactive decay, social choice and problem solving are developed and applied to everyday issues.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AISM 120 -- College Algebra  (3 units)
Description:  This course for non-mathematics majors and minors covers the basic concepts of algebra, including linear equations and inequalities, relations and functions, quadratic equations, and systems of equations.
Prerequisite(s):  two entrance units in algebra or an acceptable score on the math readiness test. Consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AISM 122 -- Biology of Organisms and the Environment  (4 units)
Description:  Building on the foundations of knowledge and skills from AISM121, this course explores the functioning of plants and animals and how they adapt to and interact with their environment. Topics include physiology, genetics, development and reproduction, and ecology. The central organizing principle of the course is evolution as a way of understanding individual organisms and the diversity of life on earth. The course will include consideration of contemporary social issues such as health care and environmental ethics.
Course includes 1 or more field trips.
Prerequisite(s):  AISM 121 or equivalent. Consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AISM 199 -- Independent Study  (1-4 units)
Description:  Qualified students working on an individual basis with professors who have agreed to supervise such work.
Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
May be repeated:  an unlimited number of times.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

AISM 201 -- Statistical Techniques for Problems of the Modern World  (3 units)
Description:  This course covers the basic techniques of descriptive statistics and statistical inference, with applications related to the second-year learning community theme(s).
Prerequisite(s):  College Algebra skills.
Usually offered:  Fall.

AISM 220 -- Molecular Genetics in a Social Context: Darwin to Dolly  (3 units)
Description:  This course is intended to provide 200-level students with an integrated understanding of the genetics, biochemistry, molecular genetics and molecular biology that form the basis of the biological revolution caused by genetic engineering.
Prerequisite(s):  instructor consent for non-AIC students.
Usually offered:  Spring.

AISM 299 -- Independent Study  (1-3 units)

Prerequisite(s):  consent of instructor for non-AIC students.
May be repeated:  an unlimited number of times.

AISM 390B -- Tropical Biology: Field Course  (3 units)
Description:  This is an upper-division science field course offered to those students who have completed AISM 390A (Tropical Biology). The course will be held entirely on-site in Corcovado National Park, Costa Rica.
Prerequisite(s):  AISM 390A; physical ability to engage in extremely strenuous, unsupervised outdoor activity.
Usually offered:  Summer.

AISM 390C -- Natural History of the Sonoran Desert  (3 units)
Description:  A field and lecture course that will give the beginner and the seasoned field observer an introduction to the natural world of our region. Class experiences will include lectures, identification workshops, and field trips. Binoculars and participation in one or two 3-4 day field trips will be required.
Prerequisite(s):  completion of AIC core or UA Gen Ed or consent of instructor.
Typical structure:  3 hours laboratory, 2 hours lecture.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.
