PSYI 803 (Pre/Sum I - Summer II) -- Clinical Clerkship  (6-9 units)
Description:  Students are assigned patients in inpatient clinical settings. They obtain and record a complete history, mental status and physical examination. The students are expected to study the course of their patients and record their observations in daily progress notes. They work closely with clinical staff in the diagnosis, specific treatment, ward management and discharge planning involving their patients. In addition, they are introduced to community and legal services for the chronically ill, the suicidal and the violent patient. Those students wishing to take an additional three-, four-, or six-week clerkship in psychiatry may elect to do so (PSYI 810).
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
May be repeated:  for a total of 12 units of credit.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

PSYI 816A (Pre/Sum I - Summer II) -- Geriatric Neuropsychiatry  (1-16 units)
Description:  To provide students with a directed clinical experience in a field that represents the convergence of internal medicine, psychiatry, and neurology. The student will develop proficiency in obtaining a complete neuropsychiatric history and performing a directed examination (mental status, cognitive screening, and neurological screening) in the geriatric patient. As well as become knowledgeable about major psychiatric disorders, personality disorders, and neuropsychiatric disorders that affect the elderly patient. The student will become knowledgeable about psychopharmacologic treatment for these disorders, and familiar with other treatment modalities, including ECT and psychotherapy.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  Completion of PSYI 803.
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.

PSYI 816D -- Consultation-Liasion-Psychosomatic Medicine  (1-10 units)
Description:  Refine history taking and mental status examination skills. Further develop skills in establishing patient rapport, conducting psychiatric interviews and making a diagnostic formulation. Identifying the patient’s emotional response and its interaction with their medical illness. Gain experience in the management of chronically ill patients with both co-existing psychiatric and medical processes in the general hospital setting. Prioritize the evaluation of acute and chronic conditions. Judiciously select diagnostic tests. Develop post-hospital continuing care plans. Understand the respective roles of psychiatry, the primary service and social work in a coordinated team effort to provide patient care. Objectives: By the end of the elective…students are expected to demonstrate proficiency with a psychiatric interview and mental status examination. Students are expected to demonstrate well developed skills in developing rapport, obtaining the necessary data and developing a formulation to explain the patient’s difficulties. Students are to have functioned as a member of the psychiatric team and interacted effectively with other health care providers at Mayo Clinic.
Grading:  Medical grades are awarded for this course: S P F K.
Prerequisite(s):  3rd & 4th-year medical students with successful completion of 1st & 2nd-years of medical school and PSYI 813C (Limited to University of Arizona medical students. No other visiting medical students will be considered).
Usually offered:  Fall, Spring.
